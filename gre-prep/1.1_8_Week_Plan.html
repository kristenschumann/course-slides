Getting Started

A flexible plan, not a rigid schedule

This plan is designed around 8 weeks, but it adapts. If you have 6 weeks, compress the early phases. If you have 12 weeks, take more time on weak areas.

Consistency beats perfection. Work through the material at whatever pace fits your life. The structure stays the same whether you move faster or slower.

0:15 - 1:00 | Set expectations. This plan is a framework, not a rigid schedule. What matters is working through material in the right order. Faster or slower is fine. Consistency is what separates students who improve from those who plateau.

Weeks 1-2

Foundations and diagnostic

Learn the test

  • Take a diagnostic practice test (ETS PowerPrep) to set your baseline.
  • GRE structure, scoring, and how to use practice tests.
  • Set up your error log and calculator strategy.

Build foundations

  • Math foundations: integers, fractions, decimals, percents, absolute value, estimation.
  • Start vocabulary building with roots, prefixes, and suffixes.
  • Learn the calculator vs. scratch paper tradeoffs.
1:00 - 2:30 | Weeks 1-2 are about learning the test itself and building arithmetic foundations. Take a diagnostic test on day one. It will feel hard, and that is normal. The score is your baseline, not your ceiling. Then work through Module 1: GRE structure, scoring, practice test strategy, error log setup, and all the math foundations (integers through quick conversions). Start vocabulary from day one using the roots and prefixes lesson.

Weeks 3-4

Core math and verbal strategy

Quantitative

  • Number properties: exponents, roots, primes, divisibility, even/odd logic.
  • Algebra fundamentals: refresher, ratios, percent change, mixture problems.

Verbal

  • Time management and sentence equivalence strategy.
  • Signal words for text completion questions.
  • Continue daily vocabulary practice.

Practice test 2. Take your second full-length test at the end of Week 4. Compare to your diagnostic and update your error log.

2:30 - 4:00 | Weeks 3-4 are where the real learning begins. On the quant side, work through Module 2 (number properties) and start Module 3 (algebra). On the verbal side, learn time management, SE strategy, and signal words from Module 2's verbal lessons. Take practice test 2 at the end of week 4 to measure progress and identify where to focus next. Your error log is critical here.

Weeks 5-6

Advanced topics and reading

Algebra and word problems

Complete Module 3: distance/rate/time, work rates, overlapping sets, quadratics, systems of equations.

Counting and probability

Module 5 math: counting principles, permutations, combinations, overlapping sets, probability.

Geometry

Module 6: coordinate geometry, angles, triangles, circles, special right triangles.

Reading comprehension

Module 5 verbal: reading approach, passage mapping, question types, paired passages.

Practice test 3. Take it mid-way through this block. Your score should be climbing. Focus review on your weakest areas.

4:00 - 5:30 | Weeks 5-6 are the heaviest content weeks. You are covering the remaining quant topics (algebra completion, counting/probability, geometry) plus starting reading comprehension. This is a lot of material, so be strategic. If you are strong in geometry, move faster. If algebra word problems are tough, spend more time there. Take practice test 3 mid-way through to calibrate. Your error log should be guiding your priorities by now.

Week 7

Data interpretation and critical reasoning

New material

  • Module 4: mean, median, mode, distributions, graphs, DI strategy.
  • Critical reasoning: inference, premises, strengthen/weaken, assumptions.

Targeted review

  • Review your error log and revisit your weakest topics.
  • Redo drills on any concept where you scored below 70%.
  • Take practice test 4 at the end of the week.
5:30 - 6:45 | Week 7 covers the last new content: data interpretation (Module 4) and critical reasoning (the rest of Module 5 verbal). At the same time, you should be reviewing weak areas from your error log. This is where targeted study pays off the most. Take practice test 4 to see where you stand heading into the final week.

Week 8

Final prep and test day

  • Analytical writing. Learn the AWA overview and issue essay strategy. Practice one timed essay.
  • Final practice test. Simulate real conditions. Use your score to set expectations.
  • Light review only. No new material. Revisit reference sheets and your error log.
  • Test day prep. Managing anxiety, the test day checklist, and score prediction tools.
6:45 - 7:45 | Week 8 is about finishing strong, not cramming. Cover Module 8: test anxiety management, the AWA overview and issue essay strategy, and the test day checklist. Take one final practice test under real conditions. Otherwise, keep it light. Review reference sheets, revisit your error log, but do not try to learn new concepts. You want to walk into test day confident, not exhausted.

Adjusting Your Plan

Adapt based on your diagnostic score

Diagnostic 150+

Compress the foundations

You already have the basics. Skim Module 1 math foundations quickly and jump into core topics by end of Week 1. Spend your extra time on advanced topics and mixed practice.

Diagnostic below 145

Extend the foundations

Spend 3 full weeks on foundations and Module 1 math. Move slowly through arithmetic before tackling algebra. Consider a 10-12 week timeline instead.

Your error log is your compass. After every practice test, let the data tell you where to spend your time.

7:45 - 8:45 | This slide is important because not everyone starts from the same place. If your diagnostic is 150 or above, you can compress weeks 1-2 and move faster into core content. If you score below 145, slow down on foundations. Spend more time on arithmetic and consider extending to 10-12 weeks. Either way, your error log should guide every decision about where to focus. The plan is a framework, not a prescription.

Key Takeaways

Your 8-week plan

  • 1. Weeks 1-2: Take a diagnostic, learn the test, and build math foundations.
  • 2. Weeks 3-4: Core number properties, algebra basics, and verbal strategy.
  • 3. Weeks 5-6: Advanced math, geometry, reading comprehension, and counting/probability.
  • 4. Week 7: Data interpretation, critical reasoning, and targeted weak-area review.
  • 5. Week 8: Writing prep, final practice test, and test day readiness.
  • 6. Adjust the pace based on your diagnostic score and error log.
8:45 - 9:15 | Recap the full plan. Weeks 1-2 foundations. Weeks 3-4 core skills. Weeks 5-6 advanced topics. Week 7 data interpretation and review. Week 8 writing and test day. The plan adapts to you. Use your diagnostic and error log to guide your pace. Consistency is what makes this work.
