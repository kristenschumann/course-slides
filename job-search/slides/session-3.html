ATS Myths vs. Reality

Common Myths

What the internet tells you

  • Fancy formatting gets you rejected
  • You need exact keyword matches
  • ATS systems are impossible to beat
The Reality

What the data shows

  • Simple formatting helps, but content matters more
  • Mirror the posting's key terms naturally. Use 'demand gen' if the posting says 'demand gen,' not just 'pipeline generation.'
  • ATS is a filter, not a judge. You can optimize for it.

Job Search Bootcamp

The Golden Rule

Every bullet on your resume should answer: "So what?"

Recruiters don't care what you did. They care what happened because you did it. Lead with outcomes. Quantify wherever possible.

Before

Marketing Manager

  • Managed social media accounts
  • Worked on email campaigns
  • Assisted with content creation
After

Growth Marketing Manager

  • Grew social following 340% in 6 months, driving 12K monthly site visits
  • Built email nurture sequence that converted 23% of free trial users to paid
  • Launched content hub generating 45 SQLs/month within first quarter
11:30 - 13:00 | Key insight. Pause. Let it land. Walk through the before/after examples. COACH: "This is the single most important resume principle. Every bullet should answer 'so what?' Look at the difference. Left side: 'Managed social media accounts.' Right side: 'Grew social following 340% in 6 months, driving 12K monthly site visits.' Same person, same work. One gets interviews, one doesn't." TRANSITION: "Let's test this right now."

Job Search Bootcamp

Gap Analysis Tips

Do
  • Use the specific job posting, not a generic description
  • Ask AI to prioritize gaps by importance
  • Keep your voice. Edit AI suggestions, don't copy-paste.
Avoid
  • Running the same resume against 20 postings without editing
  • Keyword stuffing (recruiters notice)
  • Letting AI invent experience you don't have
27:00 - 28:30 | Quick tips. Keep it practical. COACH: "Three dos and three don'ts. The biggest mistake is copy-pasting AI output without editing. AI gets you 80% there. The last 20% is your voice and your judgment."

About Section Formula

Section Purpose
Hook Bold statement that grabs attention
Story What you do and results you deliver
Proof Key achievements and metrics
CTA What you're looking for

Story: "I started in B2B content and spent 7 years moving up the funnel — from content to demand gen to owning the full growth stack at three startups."

Three Formats That Work

The Lesson Learned

Share a mistake or insight from your career. What did you learn? Keep it to 5-8 sentences.

The Hot Take

A respectful opinion about your industry. Something you believe that others might debate.

The Breakdown

Take a public example (product launch, company decision) and analyze it in 3-4 points.
