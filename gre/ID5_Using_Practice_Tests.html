Using Practice Tests
in Your Study Plan

0:00 - 0:30 | Hook: Practice tests are the most valuable tool in your GRE prep, but only if you use them right. Take them too early and you'll get discouraged. Take them without reviewing and you'll waste the learning opportunity.

Timing

When to Take Practice Tests

Week 1

Diagnostic Test

Take your first practice test early. This establishes your baseline. Don't worry if it's lower than your target.

Every 1-2 Weeks

Regular Practice Tests

Test consistently to track progress. Some people test weekly, others every two weeks. Find a rhythm that works for you.

Final Week

Final Check

One last test a few days before your real GRE. Use this as a confidence check, not a cram session.

Target: About 4-6 practice tests over 8 weeks

Key tip: Don't take practice tests when exhausted or sick. You want each test to reflect your actual current ability.

Choosing Materials

Which Practice Tests to Use

Official ETS PowerPrep

The Gold Standard

  • Real retired questions from actual GRE tests
  • Simulates section-adaptive format accurately
  • Best predictor of your actual test day score
  • 2 free tests available (use the timed version)

Additional tests: ~$40 each (POWERPREP Plus)

Third-Party Tests

Supplemental Practice

  • + Good for building stamina and practicing question types
  • + More tests available if you need extra practice
  • ! May differ in style or difficulty from real GRE
  • ! Don't rely on them for accurate score prediction

The Real Learning

How to Review Practice Tests

Most students take a test, look at the score, and move on. That's a waste. The real learning happens in the review.

1

Review every wrong answer

For each question you got wrong, figure out why. Was it a content gap? A careless mistake? Running out of time? Misreading the question?

2

Also review questions you guessed on

If you got a question right but weren't sure why, review it. Lucky guesses don't help you on test day.

3

Log your errors

Put each wrong answer in your error log with the category of mistake. Over time, patterns will emerge.

4

Identify your 2-3 biggest weaknesses

After each practice test, ask: what 2-3 things would have the biggest impact on my score if I improved them?

5

Adjust your study plan

Those 2-3 weaknesses become your priorities for the next week of study.

3:30 - 5:30 | Walk through all 5 steps. The key message: review is where learning happens. Don't just look at the score and move on. Budget as much time for review as for taking the test.

Avoid These

Common Practice Test Mistakes

1

Too many tests, not enough review

If you're taking practice tests every other day, you're not leaving time to actually learn from them. Slow down.

2

Not simulating real conditions

Extra breaks, looking things up, or a noisy environment don't simulate the real test. Take at least some tests under realistic conditions.

3

Obsessing over score fluctuations

Any single test can vary by 2-4 points. Don't panic if one test is lower than the last. Look at the trend over 3-4 tests.

4

Using all official tests too early

Official ETS tests are a limited resource. Don't use them all in the first few weeks. Save some for later when they're most useful.

5:30 - 7:00 | Cover all 4 common mistakes. Key points: balance testing with review time, simulate real conditions, don't overreact to single test scores, save official tests.
