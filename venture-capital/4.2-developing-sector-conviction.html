Section 1

Why Sector Focus Is Non-Negotiable

1:15 | Transition into why generalists lose.

VC Recruiting Course

Three levels of sector knowledge

Surface Level

You read TechCrunch headlines and can name the major players. Everyone applying has this. It is not a differentiator.

Competitive Intelligence

You can name 50 companies and their competitive positions. You understand the market structure and key dynamics.

Proprietary Views

You have informed opinions on where value will accrue, grounded in patterns you have observed firsthand.

The Mistake

She abandoned the one sector where she had an unfair advantage to compete in one where thousands of people had equally shallow opinions. Ask yourself: "Where can I develop conviction fastest given what I already know?"

VC Recruiting Course

Structure and intelligence are the foundation

Market Structure

For fintech, interchange revenue (not subscription fees) drives most payment company economics. For healthtech, hospital procurement cycles run 18-24 months. Every sector has structural facts that separate insiders from tourists.

Company Intelligence

PitchBook and CB Insights are industry standard. Tracxn and Crunchbase Pro are more affordable. Build a tracker with funding history, key metrics, go-to-market motions, and investors. Update it weekly. This is not optional.

VC Recruiting Course

How two months of tracking becomes a thesis

After eight weeks tracking the AI coding assistant market, I noticed the companies that integrated directly into the IDE were retaining users at 3x the rate of standalone web tools. That pattern was not in any analyst report. It came from tracking retention proxies across 15 companies.

Pattern recognition is not magic. It is the compound interest of paying attention. The candidates who develop it are the ones who show up consistently, not the ones who binge-read for three days before an interview.

Section 4

The Conviction Stress Test

8:00 | Transition into testing your conviction.

VC Recruiting Course

If you stumble on more than one, you need more reps

  • Where is the consensus wrong? What do you believe that most people don't?
  • If you had $10M to deploy tomorrow, where would you put it and why?
  • What is the biggest risk to your thesis? What data would change your mind?
  • What macro variables impacted your sector in the last 90 days? Track first- and second-order indicators with downstream effects — this is where investors build alpha.
  • Name the top 10 companies by stage. Explain why each is positioned where it is.

VC Recruiting Course

Strong opinions, loosely held

The Right Posture

Confident in your analytical framework for understanding the sector. Flexible on the specific conclusions as new data comes in. The best VCs combine deep domain knowledge with intellectual flexibility.

The Wrong Posture

Rigid attachment to a specific prediction. Inability to update when evidence contradicts your view. Rigidity is the enemy of good investing. The Stanford Venture Capital Initiative's research confirms this.

9:00 - 9:45 | Most people get this wrong. Conviction does not mean certainty. [Click] The right posture is confident in your framework, flexible on conclusions. [Click] The wrong posture is rigid attachment to a prediction. Update your views as new data comes in.
Section 5

Making Conviction Visible

9:45 | Transition into visibility channels.

VC Recruiting Course

If no one can see your expertise, it doesn't exist

Written Content

Substack, blog, Twitter threads, LinkedIn posts. When someone Googles your name plus your sector, they should find substance.

Network Building

Sector-specific events, niche communities, Slack and Discord servers. 15 founder conversations beats 15 blog posts.

Deal Flow Contribution

Source deals informally. A focused note with a company, why it is interesting, and three risks to diligence proves everything.

The Compounding Effect

Writing attracts founders. Founder conversations deepen knowledge. Deeper knowledge improves writing. Within six months, you have a real reputation.

VC Recruiting Course

Key takeaways

  • "I'm a generalist" is a losing position for junior candidates. Partners already go wide. They need you to go deep. Pick a sector and commit to it.
  • Choose your sector strategically, not emotionally. The best sector for you is where your background gives you an advantage, where capital is flowing, and where your target firms are active.
  • Build a knowledge system, not a reading habit. The Four Layers, from market structure to proprietary POV, are a progression. Most candidates never get past Layer 2. The ones who reach Layer 4 get hired.
  • Conviction without visibility is wasted. Write publicly, build a founder network, contribute deal flow. If no one can see your expertise, it might as well not exist.
  • Conviction is not certainty. Hold strong frameworks and update your conclusions. If you cannot name the biggest risk to your own thesis, your conviction is enthusiasm with a better vocabulary.
