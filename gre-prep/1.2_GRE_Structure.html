Overview

The big picture

The GRE takes just under 2 hours (1 hr 58 min). Three scored sections, 54 questions plus one essay.

Section Questions Time
Analytical Writing 1 essay 30 min
Verbal Reasoning 1 12 questions 18 min
Verbal Reasoning 2 15 questions 23 min
Quantitative Reasoning 1 12 questions 21 min
Quantitative Reasoning 2 15 questions 26 min

No scheduled breaks. Build up your stamina during practice to stay focused for the full two hours.

0:30 - 1:30 | Walk through the full test structure. 5 sections, about 2 hours total. The table shows exactly what to expect on test day. No scheduled breaks.

Section 1

Analytical writing

You get one essay task with 30 minutes to complete it. The prompt presents an issue or claim, and your job is to take a position and support your argument with reasoning and examples.

Key Idea

Clear structure, logical reasoning, and relevant examples matter more than fancy vocabulary.

Scoring

Scored 0 to 6 in half-point increments, separately from Verbal and Quant.

Many programs weight it less heavily, but a very low score can raise questions.

1:30 - 3:00 | Explain the writing section. One essay, 30 minutes, scored 0-6. Emphasize that clear structure matters more than vocabulary. Separate score from Verbal/Quant.

Verbal Reasoning

Three question types

Two Verbal sections: 12 questions in 18 min, then 15 questions in 23 min. 27 questions total.

Reading Comprehension

About 50% of Verbal.

Read passages and answer questions about main ideas, inferences, and arguments.

Text Completion

About 25% of Verbal.

Fill in 1, 2, or 3 blanks with the right words using context clues.

Sentence Equivalence

About 25% of Verbal.

One blank, six choices. Pick TWO that create the same meaning.

3:00 - 5:00 | Walk through the three verbal question types. Reading Comp is the biggest chunk at ~50%. Text Completion and Sentence Equivalence each about 25%. Key skills: vocabulary and careful reading for context clues.

Quantitative Reasoning

Four question types

Two Quant sections: 12 questions in 21 min, then 15 questions in 26 min. 27 questions total. On-screen calculator available.

Quantitative Comparison

Compare Quantity A vs B. Determine which is greater, equal, or cannot be determined.

Problem Solving

Standard math problems. Multiple choice with one or more correct answers.

Data Interpretation

Charts, graphs, and tables. Often come in sets of 2-3 questions about the same data.

Numeric Entry

Type your answer. No choices means no process of elimination.

5:00 - 7:00 | Cover the four quant question types. QC is unique to the GRE. Problem Solving is standard math. Data Interpretation uses charts/graphs. Numeric Entry has no answer choices. Content areas: Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Data Analysis.

How It Works

Section-level adaptive testing

Your performance on the first section of each type determines the difficulty of the second section.

Do Well on Section 1

Your second section is harder, but getting questions right in the harder section is worth more toward your final score.

This is where high scores come from.

Struggle on Section 1

Your second section is easier, but even getting most questions right won't get you as high a score.

The scoring ceiling is lower.

Important

Take your time on first sections. If running low on time, guess rather than leave questions blank.

7:00 - 7:30 | Explain the adaptive mechanism. Performance on Section 1 determines difficulty of Section 2. Doing well unlocks harder questions worth more points. Key strategy: don't rush Section 1.

Summary

GRE at a glance

  • Three sections. Writing (1 essay), Verbal (27 Qs), and Quant (27 Qs).
  • Under 2 hours. 30 min writing, 41 min verbal, 47 min quant.
  • Section-adaptive. First section performance sets difficulty of your second section.
  • Scoring. Verbal and Quant each 130-170. Writing scored 0-6 separately.
  • Strategy. Take your time on first sections to unlock the higher score ceiling.
