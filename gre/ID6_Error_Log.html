The Problem

Every Wrong Answer Is Information

The Common Trap
1

Make a mistake

2

Feel bad about it

3

Glance at the right answer

4

Move on

5

Make the SAME mistake a week later

The Key Insight

Most students make the same 3-5 types of mistakes over and over.

  • Maybe they always mess up percent change
  • Maybe they pick trap answers on inference
  • Maybe they rush and make careless errors

If you had a list of the exact 10 things costing you the most points, wouldn't you study those? Your error log creates that list.

0:30 - 2:00 | Explain the common trap cycle that most students fall into. Contrast with the key insight that most errors are repetitive. Lead into the error log as the solution.

The Template

What to Record for Every Wrong Answer

Field
What to Write
Date & Source
When it happened, from which drill or practice test
Topic
What type of question was this?
What Went Wrong
Your actual mistake, be specific
Error Type
Content gap (C), Process error (P), or Strategy error (S)
What to Remember
The rule or approach to use next time
Redo Date
When you'll try this problem again (optional but powerful)

Tip You don't need to write a novel. A few sentences is enough. The act of writing forces you to actually think about what went wrong.

2:00 - 3:00 | Walk through each field in the error log template. Emphasize that entries should be short and specific. The act of writing itself is a learning tool.

Error Types

Three Categories of Mistakes

C

Content Gaps

  • C1 Didn't know the concept
  • C2 Knew it but applied wrong
  • C3 Didn't know the vocab
P

Process Errors

  • P1 Misread the question
  • P2 Calculation mistake
  • P3 Ran out of time
  • P4 Changed correct to wrong
S

Strategy Errors

  • S1 Didn't use POE well
  • S2 Picked trap answer
  • S3 Didn't check work
  • S4 Wrong approach

Fix: Study the underlying concepts

Fix: Slow down and be careful

Fix: Review question strategies

3:00 - 3:30 | Walk through the three error categories. Content gaps mean you need to study concepts. Process errors mean you need to slow down. Strategy errors mean you need to review techniques.

Real Examples

What Error Log Entries Look Like

Content Gap (C1) Jan 13

Exponents Drill, #6

Thought x⁰ = 0 instead of x⁰ = 1

Remember, any non-zero number to the 0 power equals 1

Process Error (P2) Jan 17

Practice Test 1, QC #8

Forgot to consider negative numbers

Remember, for QC always test positive, negative, zero, and fractions

Strategy Error (S2) Jan 16

SE Drill, #4

Picked words that fit individually but weren't synonyms

Remember, SE answers must be synonyms creating the same meaning

Content Gap (C3) Jan 16

TC Practice Set, #7

Missed contrast signal "although"

Remember, "although," "however," "despite" signal contrast

Weekly Review

Finding Patterns in Your Errors

Once you have 15-20 entries, patterns emerge

1

Count by error type

How many C vs P vs S? This tells you what to fix.

2

Count by topic

More geometry? More SE? This tells you where to focus.

3

Look for repeats

Same note appearing multiple times? That's priority #1.

6:00 - 7:00 | Explain the redo system as spaced repetition. Log the error, set a redo date for one week, then redo without notes. If right, move to monthly review. If wrong, redo in 3-4 days. This is how learning gets cemented.
