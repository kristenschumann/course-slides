Target Scores

What Score Do You Need?

Score What It Means
5.0+ Strong score. Unlikely to be a concern for any program.
4.5 Comfortable margin. Above ~80% of test-takers.
4.0 Safe for most programs. Around the 60th percentile.
<4.0 Potential red flag, especially if rest of application doesn't show strong writing.

Who Can Hit 4.0+ Easily?

If you're a native English speaker with decent writing skills, you can probably hit 4.0+ with minimal prep.

If English is your second language or writing isn't your strength, budget more time.

2:00 - 3:00 | What's good enough? 5.0+ is strong. 4.5 is comfortable. 4.0 is safe. Below 4.0 is potentially a red flag. Native English speakers can usually hit 4.0 with minimal prep.

The Essentials

Minimum Prep for Everyone

Even if writing isn't your priority, do at least this much. It's maybe 2-3 hours total.

1

Know the Task

Analyze an Issue: Give your opinion on a statement. You'll have 30 minutes to write a persuasive essay.

2

Know the Scoring

ETS wants clear organization, specific examples, and logical development. Not creative writing or perfect grammar.

3

Practice Once, Timed

Write at least one essay under timed conditions. Don't see the format for the first time on test day.

Structure tip: Intro-body-body-body-conclusion works fine. Don't try to be fancy. Clear beats creative.

3:00 - 4:30 | Three minimum prep steps: 1) Know the task (Analyze an Issue), 2) Know scoring criteria, 3) Practice at least once under timed conditions. Have a basic structure ready. That's 2-3 hours.

Extra Preparation

When to Do More

Do more prep if:

Your diagnostic essay score is 3.5 or below

Writing in English is challenging for you

Your target programs emphasize writing ability

Your other materials (personal statement, writing sample) also need work

If any apply, budget time to:

1.

Study sample essays at different score levels

2.

Practice 3-4 essays of each type

3.

Get feedback from someone else

4.

Focus on clear structure and specific examples

The writing section isn't a mystery. ETS tells you exactly what they want.

4:30 - 5:30 | Do more if: diagnostic below 3.5, English is challenging, programs emphasize writing, or other materials need work. If so: study sample essays, practice 3-4 of each type, get feedback, focus on structure.
