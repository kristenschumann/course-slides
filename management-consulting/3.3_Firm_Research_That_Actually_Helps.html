Generic vs. Differentiated Answers

Generic Answer

Tells the interviewer you skimmed a website.

"I'm interested in BCG because you do great work in technology and I'm passionate about tech."

Differentiated Answer

Shows you understand the firm's direction and connect it to your background.

"I'm drawn to BCG's Climate and Sustainability practice. With my engineering background, I'm excited about BCG's customizable learning paths for energy transition and digital strategy."

0:45 - 1:15 | The first answer could apply to any firm. The second references a specific practice, connects to personal background, and shows real career thinking. That's the gap we're closing.

Five Research Categories

  • Culture and Work Style. How the firm operates day to day and what it values.

MBB Culture Comparison

McKinsey

Most formal. Strong hierarchy. "Obligation to Dissent" culture means you're expected to speak up, even with partners.

BCG

Intellectual, academic culture. Customizable learning paths let you build expertise in AI, sustainability, or digital strategy.

Bain

Most social and collaborative. Team-oriented staffing model. Generally better work-life balance (except their massive PE practice).

1:55 - 2:45 | These are interview ammunition. For McKinsey, say something like: "Your Obligation to Dissent culture stands out. In my last role, I pushed back on a senior leader's strategy and it led to a better outcome." That's specific, connected to experience, and memorable.

When You Specialize Matters

The three firms handle specialization very differently, and saying the wrong thing signals you don't understand their model.

McKinsey and BCG move consultants into specific practices within 1-2 years. Bain keeps you generalist until partner level, 2-5 years later than the other firms.

Match your stated career goals to the firm's actual structure. Saying "I want to specialize immediately" to Bain is a red flag.

3:30 - 4:00 | This one surprises candidates. If you say "I want to specialize in healthcare immediately" to a Bain interviewer, you've just signaled you don't understand their model. Match your career goals to the firm's actual structure.

Questions That Unlock Insights

  • "What surprised you most about working here?"
  • "How does this firm differ from others you considered?"
  • "When did you specialize, and how much choice did you have?"
  • "What's the culture like in [specific office]?"

Then use what you learn in your interviews. Reference a real conversation, a specific person and practice, and connect it to your career goals. See Resource 2.4 for the full coffee chat playbook.

4:50 - 5:20 | These questions unlock research you can't find anywhere else. Then use it: "When I spoke with Sarah about BCG's digital transformation practice, she described creative freedom I haven't seen at other firms." That answer is impossible to fake.

Your Research Timeline

8-12 Weeks Out

Broad Research

Survey 10-20 firms. Practice areas, culture, application deadlines. Build your initial target list.

6-8 Weeks Out

Network

Start coffee chats. Gather the firsthand insights websites can't give you.

4-6 Weeks Out

Deep Dive

Top 5-8 firms. Case studies, recent projects, office-specific details. This is where answers get sharp.

2-3 Weeks Out

Draft and Practice

Write your firm-specific "Why this firm?" answers. Run them by a peer or mentor.

5:20 - 5:40 | MBA candidates: this aligns with the August recruiting calendar. Undergrads: your school's timeline drives deadlines. Experienced hires: rolling schedule, start as soon as you decide to apply.

Key Takeaways

Firm Research That Helps

Go deeper than generic. Cover culture, practice areas, career paths, recent work, and office-level details. Reading the "About Us" page is the starting line, not the finish.

MBB firms are not interchangeable. McKinsey is formal with Obligation to Dissent. BCG is intellectual with customizable learning. Bain is collaborative with the largest PE practice in the industry.

Coffee chats are your highest-leverage tool. A 20-minute conversation with a current consultant gives you insights no website can.

Start broad, then go deep. Begin 8-12 weeks out with wide research. Go deep on your top firms 4-6 weeks before applications.

5:40 - 6:00 | Four things to remember. Generic research produces generic answers. MBB firms have real differences. Coffee chats are the single best research method. And start early with a clear timeline.
