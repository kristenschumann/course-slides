Timing and Materials

When and which tests to use

Testing Schedule

After Week 1: Diagnostic test (your baseline).

Every 1-2 weeks: Regular practice tests.

Final week: One last test before the real GRE.

Target

About 4-6 practice tests over 8 weeks.

2 free tests available. Additional tests ~$40 each.

Third-Party Tests

Great for extra practice, but may differ in style. Don't rely on them for score prediction.

The Real Learning

How to review practice tests

Most students look at the score and move on. The real learning happens in the review.

  • Review every wrong answer. Figure out why: content gap, careless mistake, time pressure, or misreading.
  • Also review lucky guesses. If you got it right but weren't sure why, it still needs review.
  • Log errors and identify weaknesses. After each test, find your 2-3 biggest gaps and prioritize them.
  • Adjust your study plan. Those weaknesses become your priorities for the next week.
3:30 - 5:30 | Walk through the review process. Review every wrong answer and lucky guesses. Log errors, identify 2-3 biggest weaknesses, adjust study plan. Budget as much time for review as for taking the test.

Avoid These

Common practice test mistakes

Too Many Tests, Not Enough Review

Testing every other day leaves no time to learn from results. Slow down.

Not Simulating Real Conditions

Extra breaks or looking things up don't reflect the real test. Simulate realistic conditions.

Obsessing Over Score Fluctuations

Any single test can vary by 2-4 points. Look at the trend over 3-4 tests.

Using Official Tests Too Early

ETS tests are limited. Save some for later when they are most useful for score prediction.

5:30 - 7:00 | Cover all 4 common mistakes. Balance testing with review time. Simulate real conditions. Don't overreact to single test scores. Save official tests for when they matter most.

Summary

Your practice test strategy

  • Take a diagnostic early, then test every 1-2 weeks.
  • Prioritize ETS PowerPrep for accurate score prediction. Use third-party tests for extra practice.
  • Spend as much time reviewing as taking the test. The review is where learning happens.
  • Log errors and adjust your study plan based on what you learn from each test.
  • Simulate real conditions at least sometimes. Build test-day stamina.
  • Look at trends, not single scores. Any one test can vary by 2-4 points.
