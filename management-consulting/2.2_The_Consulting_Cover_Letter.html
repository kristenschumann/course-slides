The Three-Paragraph Structure

Why You, Why Consulting, Why This Firm

1:30 - 1:40 | Transition. Every firm expects this structure. Learn it, use it, don't deviate.

Break Into Management Consulting

Three Questions, Three Paragraphs

Paragraph Question What to Include
Why You What makes you a strong candidate? Lead with a differentiator: a project, a result, a challenge. Open with a hook, not a generic line.
Why Consulting Why do you want this career? Tie your answer to a specific experience. Generic enthusiasm loses credibility.
Why This Firm Why this firm specifically? Firm research, values, recent projects, conversations with consultants. Rewrite for every application.
1:40 - 2:30 | Walk through each paragraph. Why You: lead with substance, not "I am writing to apply." Why Consulting: ground it in experience, not generic motivation. Why This Firm: must be rewritten for every application. Specificity creates credibility.

Weak vs. Strong Openings

Weak

Generic phrasing that could apply to any firm.

"I am interested in working at a top consulting firm like BCG."

Strong

References firm values and ties them to experience.

"BCG's emphasis on creative problem-solving resonates with my experience leading data-driven projects in fast-paced environments."

2:30 - 3:00 | Compare weak vs. strong "Why This Firm" paragraphs. The weak one could apply to any firm. The strong one references specific BCG values and connects them to the candidate's background.

Break Into Management Consulting

Firm-Specific Techniques

  • Reference specific projects or cases the firm has worked on
  • Name people you've spoken with and what you learned
  • Cite specific firm values and connect them to your experience
  • Mention recent firm publications or reports

The entire letter must fit on one page. Going over one page is the cardinal sin of consulting cover letters.

3:00 - 3:30 | Four techniques for customization. Specificity creates credibility. And the entire letter fits on one page. If you can't structure your story in one page, that's a signal you can't communicate concisely.

Worked Example

A strong "Why You" paragraph in practice

3:30 - 3:40 | Transition to worked example.

Break Into Management Consulting

Strong Opening Paragraph

"In my role as Operations Lead at a SaaS startup, I identified a bottleneck in our customer onboarding process costing us 15% annual revenue growth. I built a data model, proposed a redesign, and led a cross-functional team to implement it. Six months later, onboarding time dropped by 40% and revenue growth hit 22%."

This paragraph has a specific challenge, a quantified result, and a clear connection to consulting. You could read it in 20 seconds and know exactly who this candidate is.

3:40 - 4:15 | Walk through the example. Notice the quantification: 40% reduction beats "improved the process." $200K savings beats "optimized procurement." Consulting is a numbers-driven business.

Key Takeaways

What to Remember

Three paragraphs, three questions: Why You, Why Consulting, Why This Firm. The third must be rewritten for every application.

Lead with substance: Your opening sentence is the most valuable real estate in the letter. Don't waste it on "I am writing to apply."

Quantify everything: Numbers create credibility. "Increased revenue by $50K" beats "helped grow the business" every time.

One page, no exceptions: If you can't structure your story in one page, that tells the recruiter you can't communicate concisely.

5:45 - 6:00 | Four key takeaways. Your cover letter is your writing sample, your personality test, and your chance to control the narrative. Treat it like the strategic document it is.
