Prediction

How to predict your score

Your real GRE score will likely be within 2-4 points of your most recent PowerPrep score. Some score higher due to adrenaline, others lower due to nerves. Both are normal.

Most Predictive

Recent ETS PowerPrep tests. Your Week 7 score matters more than Week 2.

Less Predictive

Third-party tests. Use for practice, not score estimation.

0:05 - 1:30 | Explain score prediction. The key point: recent official practice tests are the best predictor, within a 2-4 point range. Third-party tests are less accurate. If scores are consistent across 2-3 tests, that is likely close to true ability. Tip: if your PowerPrep scores vary by more than 4 points between attempts, focus on the conditions that caused the swing (fatigue, timing pressure, specific question types) rather than averaging the scores.

Test Day

Two decisions after you finish

Accept or Cancel

You see unofficial V and Q scores immediately. You can cancel right there, and no one will ever see them.

Send Scores

If you accept, choose which schools receive scores. Up to 4 free on test day.

Advice

Unless something went terribly wrong, accept. You can retake and use ScoreSelect later.

1:30 - 2:30 | Explain the two decisions students face immediately after finishing the test. The recommendation is almost always to accept. Canceling is irreversible and usually unnecessary because of ScoreSelect.

Score Reporting

How ScoreSelect works

If you take the GRE multiple times, ScoreSelect lets you control which scores schools see.

Most Recent

Send only your latest test scores.

Any Single Date

Send scores from whichever test date you choose.

All Scores

Send your complete testing history.

Note

Some schools request all scores. Check their policies. Some schools superscore (best Verbal from one test, best Quant from another).

2:30 - 4:00 | Explain ScoreSelect options. This is a major advantage of the GRE. Students choose at the time they send scores, not on test day. Schools won't see how many times you tested unless you send all scores.

Next Steps

Three score scenarios

Hit Target

You Met Your Goal

Send scores to schools and focus on other parts of your application.

Close

Within 2-3 Points

Consider whether the gap matters. A 158 vs. 160 rarely makes a difference.

Below

Significantly Below

Retake, adjust your school list, or strengthen other application components.

Timeline

Official scores arrive in 8-10 days. You can retake every 21 days, up to 5 times per year.

4:00 - 5:30 | Walk through the three scenarios. For most students, the answer is not to panic. A few points rarely make or break an application. Retaking makes sense only if you have clear reason to expect improvement. Key retake rule: only retake if you can identify specific, fixable weaknesses (e.g., ran out of time on Quant, blanked on vocab). If you performed at your practice test level, a retake without changed preparation will likely produce the same result.

Retake Decision

Should you retake the GRE?

Within 1-2 Points

Likely not worth retaking

A 158 vs. 160 rarely changes admissions outcomes. Focus energy on essays and recommendations.

3+ Points Below

Consider retaking

Review your error patterns first. Only retake if you can identify specific, fixable weaknesses.

2+ Attempts, Plateau

Diminishing returns

If scores are flat after two takes with changed prep, focus elsewhere. Adjust your school list or strengthen other application components.

Key Question

Test-day issues vs. content gaps?

Anxiety or timing problems are fixable with practice. If you performed at your practice-test level, more prep is needed before retaking.

5:00 - 5:30 | Walk through the retake decision framework. The key distinction: test-day issues (anxiety, timing, illness) vs. content gaps. Test-day issues can improve with another attempt. Content gaps require more study before retaking.

Key Takeaways

Score prediction and next steps

  • Your recent PowerPrep score is the best predictor of your actual GRE score, within 2-4 points
  • Accept your scores on test day unless something went seriously wrong
  • ScoreSelect lets you control which scores schools see if you test multiple times
  • Retake strategically only if you had test-day issues or know specific areas to improve
  • A few points rarely matter. Focus on your complete application, not just one number
5:30 - 6:00 | Summary slide. Trust your preparation. Execute on test day and deal with next steps when you have real information.
