Pre-MBA Mindset

Balance Preparation with Rest

You've Earned a Break

The application process was intense. Rest now. The MBA will be demanding.

You Don't Need to Be an Expert

The MBA exists to teach you. You're not expected to arrive mastering finance.

Transition, Not Transformation

Focus on reasonable preparation, not unrealistic ambitions before day one.

Don't Over-Prepare

Some preparation helps. Over-preparation creates anxiety and burnout before you begin.

1:00 - 2:15 | First, the right mindset. You've earned a break. The application was intense, so decompress before diving into the next thing. You don't need to arrive as an expert. The MBA exists to teach you things you don't know. This time is for transition, not transformation. Focus on reasonable preparation. And don't over-prepare. Some prep helps, but teaching yourself the entire first semester is unnecessary and exhausting.

Practical Logistics

Handle These Before You Arrive

  • Housing: Research early, join housing boards and group chats for roommates.
  • Finances: Apply for loans, create a 2-year budget, understand scholarship conditions.
  • Work transition: Give notice, leave on good terms, wrap up projects professionally.
  • Health insurance: Understand your student coverage and handle medical needs before it changes.
2:15 - 3:45 | Practical logistics to handle. Housing: research early and join school housing boards and group chats. Finances: apply for loans, create a budget for tuition, living, and reduced income. Work transition: give appropriate notice, leave on good terms, wrap up projects professionally. Stay connected with colleagues you value. Health insurance: understand what coverage you'll have as a student and handle any medical needs before your insurance changes.

Academic Preparation

Brush Up on These Basics

Subject What to Review Top Resources
Accounting Debits, credits, financial statements Wharton Coursera course, Libby textbook
Statistics Mean, median, probability Khan Academy (free)
Excel Financial models, formulas, analysis Coursera Excel for Business
Finance Time value of money, NPV, IRR Damodaran's NYU lectures (free)

Your school's pre-term materials are highest priority. If they provide a math camp or accounting primer, start there.

3:45 - 5:30 | Some academic preparation is valuable, especially if you have gaps. Accounting: review debits, credits, and financial statements. The free Wharton Coursera course is excellent. Statistics: brush up on mean, median, probability with Khan Academy. Excel: everyone assumes you know it, so practice with financial models. Finance fundamentals: time value of money, NPV, IRR. Damodaran's free NYU lectures are exceptional. But your school's pre-term materials are the highest priority. If they provide a math camp or primer, do those first. If you already know this material well, spend your time elsewhere.

Building Relationships

Start Connecting Before Day One

Join Class Channels

Slack, WhatsApp, Discord, Facebook, LinkedIn groups. Join all of them to find where your cohort is most active.

Attend Pre-Term Events

Official and informal events for admitted students. Low-pressure opportunities to meet people before orientation.

Connect One-on-One

Reach out to interesting classmates for a virtual coffee. Most people are excited to connect.

5:30 - 7:00 | Your classmates will be some of the most valuable connections of your MBA. Start building relationships now. Join all admitted student channels. Slack, WhatsApp, Discord, LinkedIn groups. You'll figure out which one your cohort actually uses. Attend pre-term events, both official and informal. These are low-pressure ways to meet people before orientation. And connect one-on-one. If you see a classmate with an interesting background, message them. Suggest a virtual coffee. Don't stress if you're not a natural networker. You'll meet plenty of people during orientation.

Personal Development

This May Be Your Last Extended Break

Pursue interests you've been putting off. Travel, learn a skill, spend time with family, work on a personal project. The MBA will be busy.

Establish healthy habits now. Exercise, sleep, and stress management are easier to maintain than to build during the program. Revisit your goals and make sure they still feel right.

7:00 - 8:30 | This time is also for personal development. Pursue interests you've been putting off. Travel, learn a skill, spend time with family. The MBA will be busy, and this may be your last extended break for a while. Establish healthy habits now. Exercise, sleep, and stress management are easier to maintain than to build during the program. And revisit your goals. You articulated them in your application. Are they still right? Having clarity helps you make better decisions about courses, activities, and recruiting.

Setting Up for Success

Final Tips Before You Start

  • Talk to second-year students about what they wish they'd known.
  • Familiarize yourself with courses, clubs, and opportunities.
  • Understand the recruiting timeline if targeting competitive roles.
  • If considering a deferral, contact admissions early.
  • Relax. The MBA is a marathon. Recharge so you bring energy to it.
8:30 - 9:45 | A few final tips. Talk to second-year students and ask what they wish they'd done before starting. Familiarize yourself with courses, professors, and clubs so you can make better early decisions. If you're targeting consulting, banking, or tech, understand the recruiting timeline. These processes start very early. If considering a deferral, contact admissions early as policies vary. And seriously, relax. You'll work hard for two years. Use this time to recharge.

Key Takeaways

What to Remember

  • Take a break. You've earned it, and you'll need the energy later
  • Handle logistics: housing, finances, work transition, insurance
  • Brush up on accounting, statistics, and Excel, but don't overdo it
  • Join class channels and attend pre-term events to build relationships
  • Pursue personal interests and establish healthy habits
  • Reflect on your goals so you arrive with clarity
9:45 - 10:30 | Key takeaways. Take a break. You've earned it. Handle the logistics: housing, finances, work transition, insurance. Brush up on the academic basics, but don't overdo it. Join class channels and attend events to start building your network. Pursue personal interests while you have the time. And reflect on your goals so you arrive knowing what you want from the experience. You're about to embark on something exciting. Prepare well, but enjoy the journey.
