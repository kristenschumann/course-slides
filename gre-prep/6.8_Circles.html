Arcs & Sectors

The fraction principle

Central angle $= \theta\degree$ means the arc/sector is $\frac{\theta}{360}$ of the whole circle.

Arc length and sector area are fractions of the whole circle.

1:40-2:20 | The fraction principle: central angle over 360 gives the fraction of the circle. Arc length is that fraction times the circumference. Sector area is that fraction times the total area. GRE tip: simplify the fraction first. 72/360 = 1/5, 120/360 = 1/3. The GRE loves "nice" fractions, so if your fraction doesn't simplify cleanly, double-check your setup.

Arcs & Sectors

Arc length and sector area

Arc Length

$\frac{\theta}{360} \times 2\pi r$

$r = 10$, $\theta = 72\degree$: $\frac{72}{360} \times 20\pi = $ $4\pi$

Sector Area

$\frac{\theta}{360} \times \pi r^2$

$r = 10$, $\theta = 72\degree$: $\frac{72}{360} \times 100\pi = $ $20\pi$

2:20-3:00 | With r = 10 and 72 degrees: 72/360 = 1/5, so arc = 4pi, sector = 20pi.

Example 1

Working backwards to the angle

Problem

If an arc has length $6\pi$ on a circle with radius 9, what is the central angle?

Solution

$6\pi = \frac{\theta}{360} \times 2\pi(9)$

$6\pi = \frac{\theta}{360} \times 18\pi$

$\frac{\theta}{360} = \frac{1}{3}$

Answer: $\theta =$ $120\degree$

3:00-3:50 | Working backwards: given arc length and radius, solve for the central angle. Set up the equation, divide both sides by 18pi, get theta/360 = 1/3, so theta = 120 degrees.

Circle Angles

Central vs inscribed

Key Rule Inscribed angle $= \frac{1}{2} \times$ central angle (same arc)

4:00-4:30 | Central angle equals the intercepted arc. Inscribed angle equals half the intercepted arc. If both intercept the same arc, the inscribed angle is half the central angle. Central of 80 means inscribed of 40.

Circle Angles

Central vs inscribed rules

Central Angle

Vertex at center

Measure $=$ intercepted arc

Inscribed Angle

Vertex on the circle

Measure $= \frac{1}{2} \times$ intercepted arc

4:30-5:00 | Central angle: vertex at center, measure equals the intercepted arc. Inscribed angle: vertex on the circle, measure equals half the intercepted arc.

Circle Angles

Inscribed angle corollaries

Same Arc

Inscribed angles intercepting the same arc are equal.

Arc $AC = 140\degree$, inscribed angle $=$ $70\degree$

Semicircle

Inscribed in a semicircle (diameter) $=$ $90\degree$

If $AC$ is a diameter, then $\angle ABC =$ $90\degree$

5:00-6:00 | Two important corollaries. First: all inscribed angles intercepting the same arc are equal. Second: an inscribed angle in a semicircle is always 90 degrees, because it intercepts a 180-degree arc. This is Thales' theorem. GRE trap: when a problem shows a triangle inscribed in a circle with one side as the diameter, the angle opposite that side is ALWAYS 90 degrees. This unlocks Pythagorean theorem for the rest of the problem.

Example 2

Tangent and radius form a right angle

Problem

If OT is drawn from center O to point T on the tangent, with OP = 5 and PT = 12, what is OT?

Solution

Tangent $\perp$ radius at P, forming a right triangle.

$OT^2 = 5^2 + 12^2 = 25 + 144 = 169$

Answer: $OT =$ $13$ (5-12-13 triple)

6:10-7:10 | Tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency, creating a right angle. OP = 5, PT = 12, so OT is the hypotenuse of a 5-12-13 right triangle. OT = 13.

Tangent Properties

Equal tangent segments

Two tangents from the same external point are equal: $PA = PB$.

Example

What fraction of the circle is an arc of $4\pi$ when $r = 12$?

$\frac{4\pi}{24\pi} = $ $\frac{1}{6}$

Example

Two concentric circles have radii 4 and 6. What is the ring area?

$36\pi - 16\pi = $ $20\pi$

7:10-8:30 | Two tangent segments from the same external point are equal. Also two application problems: pizza slice fraction = arc/circumference = 4pi/24pi = 1/6. Concentric ring area = outer minus inner = 36pi - 16pi = 20pi.

Key Takeaways

Circles: Arcs, sectors & angles

  • The fraction principle: Arc length and sector area are $\frac{\theta}{360}$ of the circumference and area
  • Central vs. inscribed angles: Central angle equals the arc; inscribed angle equals half the arc
  • Semicircle rule: Inscribed angle in a semicircle is always $90\degree$
  • Tangent properties: Perpendicular to radius at contact point; equal segments from an external point
8:30-10:00 | Summary: use the fraction principle for arcs and sectors. Central angle = arc, inscribed = half arc, semicircle = 90 degrees. Tangent is perpendicular to radius and equal tangents from an external point.
