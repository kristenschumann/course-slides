Keep Your Introduction Concise

30-60 Seconds. Share your background, what draws you to consulting, and what interests you about their firm. Then stop talking and ask a question.

Match your story to the firm. Emphasize collaboration for Bain, intellectual curiosity for McKinsey, creative problem-solving for BCG. Highlight what aligns with the firm's values.

The Referral Ask

How to ask without being awkward

4:45 - 4:55 | Transition to referral strategy.

Break Into Management Consulting

Two Approaches to Referrals

The Indirect Approach

  • "Are referrals common at your firm?"
  • "Is there anything you could help me with to boost my application?"
  • Opens the door without pressure

The Direct Approach

  • "Would you be comfortable referring me?"
  • Use only after multiple touchpoints
  • Clear, polite, and specific
4:55 - 6:00 | Never ask for a referral in the first chat. Build the relationship first. The indirect approach plants a seed. The direct approach works after genuine relationship building. Target associates, consultants, and engagement managers. Start networking 6 months before recruiting season if possible.

After the Chat

  • Thank-you within 24 hours. Reference something specific from the conversation.
  • Jot down key takeaways. What did you learn? What surprised you? What would you change?
  • Maintain the relationship. Send check-ins every few months and share progress on your application.
6:00 - 6:30 | Follow up within 24 hours with a personalized thank-you. Write down key takeaways immediately while they're fresh. Maintain the relationship over time with periodic check-ins. Most candidates disappear after one chat.

Key Takeaways

The Coffee Chat Playbook

Prepare like it matters. Coffee chats are informal, but every conversation shapes an impression. Fifteen minutes of prep makes all the difference.

Ask smart questions and listen. The best chats get the other person talking about their experience. Your job is to show genuine curiosity, not recite your resume.

Build the relationship before asking. Never ask for a referral in the first conversation. Earn it through preparation, good questions, and follow-through.

Follow up and stay in touch. Send a personalized thank-you within 24 hours. The candidates who maintain relationships are the ones who get referrals.

6:30 - 7:00 | Recap: prepare like it matters, ask smart questions and listen, build relationships before asking for referrals, and follow up consistently.
