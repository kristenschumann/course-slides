Who to Reach Out To

Choosing the right targets for your outreach

1:45 - 1:50 | Transition. The first strategic decision is who to target. Most candidates get this wrong.

Target by Seniority Level

Junior (Priority)

Analysts and Associates respond most often, are closest to recruiting, and are the most candid.

Mid-Level

Engagement Managers offer career progression insights and project work perspective.

Senior

Partners provide industry perspective but are harder to reach. Lower priority for cold outreach.

1:50 - 2:45 | Junior consultants are your priority. They reply more, they remember what recruiting was like, they have time for a 20-minute call, and they're more candid about the actual experience. Senior people provide a broader perspective but are the hardest to reach.

Set Realistic Expectations

The math: 10 outreach emails typically yield 1 response. If your goal is 5 coffee chats, plan to send 50 emails. Volume matters alongside quality.

Students should target campus recruiters and recent alums. Experienced hires should target Managers and Partners closer to lateral hiring decisions.

Five Sourcing Channels

  • LinkedIn: Search by firm and title, filter by school, location, or connections
  • Alumni networks: Highest response rate, shared school creates instant connection
  • Firm events: On-campus presentations, info sessions, virtual recruiting events
  • Personal network: Friends, former colleagues, and classmates already in consulting
  • Referral chains: End every conversation with "Who else should I talk to?"
3:20 - 4:00 | Five channels. LinkedIn is the workhorse. Alumni network is gold because shared alma mater creates immediate connection. Firm events give you face time. Personal network is the easiest starting point. Every conversation ends with a referral ask to expand your network organically.

Writing the Outreach Email

Generic Outreach

Long, impersonal, no specific ask. Reads like a copy-paste template.

"Hi, I'm really interested in your firm. I'd love to pick your brain about consulting sometime. Let me know if you're free."

Targeted Outreach

Short, personalized, specific ask. Easy to say yes to.

"I'm a second-year analyst recruiting for consulting. I noticed you transitioned from tech into BCG's digital practice. Would you have 20 minutes for a quick call this week?"

4:00 - 5:00 | The outreach email is the highest-leverage moment. Structure: who you are (1 line), why them specifically (personalized hook), your specific ask (20 min call), polite close. 3-5 sentences total. No flattery, no "I've always admired your firm." Subject line matters too: "Fellow [School] alum, quick question about BCG" gets opened.

Coffee Chat Structure

Opening (1-2 min)

Thank them, share a quick intro, and set expectations for the conversation.

Main Talk (20-25 min)

Ask 5-7 prepared questions about their experience. Keep it conversational, not an interrogation.

Closing (2-3 min)

Share specific takeaways, thank them again, and ask for referrals to other contacts.

6:00 - 7:15 | Coffee chat structure. Opening: thank them, introduce yourself briefly, set expectations. Main conversation: ask about their personal experience, not things you could Google. Prepare 5-7 questions but use them as a guide, not a checklist. Closing: mention specific takeaways, ask "Is there anyone else you'd recommend I speak with?" Candidates who stand out set context for their questions.

Key Takeaways

Networking That Works

  • Build relationships, not connections. Networking is about learning from people at your target firms and creating genuine advocates who flag your resume.
  • Target junior consultants first. Associates respond more often, are closer to recruiting, and are more candid. Expect a 10:1 outreach-to-response ratio.
  • Keep outreach short and specific. Three to five sentences: who you are, why them, your clear ask. No flattery, no vague language.
  • Follow up, then follow through. One follow-up after a week. Thank-you within 24 hours. Stay in touch every few months with something valuable.
7:15 - 8:00 | Four key takeaways. First, networking builds advocates, not just connections. Second, target junior people first. Third, outreach should be 3-5 sentences, short, specific, personalized. Fourth, follow up once after a week, send thank-you within 24 hours, then maintain the relationship every few months. This is the playbook that works.
