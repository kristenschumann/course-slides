The Negotiation Map

What moves and what does not

1:30 - 1:35 | Section break. Transition into negotiation guidance.

Break Into Management Consulting

Non-Negotiable vs. Negotiable

Non-Negotiable

  • Base salary (fixed by level)
  • Bonus formula (firm-wide policy)
  • Title / level (standard tracks)

Negotiable

  • Signing bonus (primary lever)
  • Relocation stipend ($5K-$10K base)
  • Start date
  • Entry level (experienced hires only)
1:35 - 3:00 | Left column is locked. Right column is where you have leverage. Signing bonus is the primary lever, a one-time cost. Standard range is 5-20% of base. Firms rarely increase by more than 25-30%. Three things that give negotiating power: competing offers, forgone comp, and off-cycle timing. Experienced hires can negotiate entry level. Two mistakes: trying to negotiate base at MBB and fabricating competing offers.

How to Make the Ask

  • Be data-driven: "Given my forgone bonus of $X, I'd like to discuss the signing bonus"
  • Make one consolidated ask because going back multiple times erodes goodwill
  • Use a phone call, not email, for the actual negotiation conversation
3:00 - 3:30 | Be collaborative and data-driven. Provide a concrete justification. One ask, one call. Going back repeatedly looks disorganized and erodes goodwill before day one.

Choosing Between Offers

A structured framework for making the decision

3:30 - 3:35 | Section break. Transition to decision framework.

Break Into Management Consulting

The 6-Factor Decision Scorecard

Culture Fit

McKinsey: intellectual rigor. BCG: creative curiosity. Bain: team-first energy. Office matters as much as firm.

Exit Alignment

PE exit: lean Bain. Fortune 500 leadership: lean McKinsey. Startups: all three are strong launching pads.

Specialty Match

McKinsey: public sector, tech. BCG: digital transformation. Bain: PE due diligence. Align with your sector interest.

Also weigh: work-life balance, compensation trajectory, and location preference. Assign weights totaling 100%, score 1-5, multiply and sum.

3:35 - 5:15 | Six factors: culture, exit opportunities, specialty match, work-life balance, compensation trajectory, and location. Culture varies by office, not just firm. Bain staffs locally, BCG regionally, McKinsey globally. Base salaries nearly identical across MBB. Use a weighted scorecard: assign weights totaling 100%, score each offer 1-5, multiply and sum. The math forces honesty about priorities.

Traps to Avoid

  • Prestige bias: choosing the "most impressive" name over the best fit for your goals
  • Compensation anchoring: obsessing over a $5K signing bonus gap that is less than 2% of two-year earnings
  • Recency bias: favoring whichever firm had the best sell weekend or warmest recruiter email
5:15 - 5:40 | Three traps derail smart candidates. Prestige bias: picking the name over the fit. Compensation anchoring: agonizing over a small signing gap. Recency bias: favoring the firm with the best sell weekend. Your five-year plan should drive the decision.

Key Takeaways

What to Remember

  • Signing bonus is your lever. Base salary at MBB is not negotiable. Come with a data-driven justification.
  • Competing offers and forgone comp are the two strongest negotiating tools. Quantify them.
  • Experienced hires: negotiating entry level is the highest-ROI move available to you.
  • Use a weighted scorecard across culture, exits, specialty, travel, growth, and location to compare offers.
  • Choose for your five-year plan, not the name that sounds most impressive at a dinner party.
