Session 1 Resource

AI Job-Matching Prompt

A copy-paste prompt template that analyzes job postings against your Role-Fit Scorecard and returns ranked matches with reasoning.

How to Use This Prompt

  1. Copy the prompt below into ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI assistant
  2. Fill in the bracketed sections with your personal information
  3. Paste 5-10 job postings for analysis
  4. Review the ranked results and add top matches to your target list
  5. Iterate: adjust your criteria if results don't feel right

The Prompt

This is a three-part prompt. Copy the entire thing, fill in your details, and paste it into your AI tool along with job postings at the end.

PART 1: CONTEXT SETTING

I'm conducting a targeted job search and need your help analyzing job postings against my personal criteria. Here's my background:

Current/most recent role: [YOUR CURRENT ROLE, e.g., "Senior Marketing Manager at a B2B SaaS company"]
Years of experience: [NUMBER]
Key skills: [LIST 5-8 CORE SKILLS, e.g., "demand generation, content strategy, marketing automation, data analysis, team management"]
Industry background: [YOUR INDUSTRIES, e.g., "tech, SaaS, fintech"]
Target role type: [WHAT YOU'RE LOOKING FOR, e.g., "Director of Marketing or VP Marketing at a growth-stage startup"]

PART 2: MY ROLE-FIT SCORECARD

Rate each job against these criteria on a 1-5 scale:

FUNCTION FIT (weight: 30%)
- Role matches my target title and level: [MUST-HAVE / NICE-TO-HAVE]
- Uses my core skills daily: [LIST YOUR TOP 3 SKILLS]
- Growth trajectory aligns with my 3-year goal: [YOUR GOAL, e.g., "VP Marketing"]
- Dealbreakers: [e.g., "No pure individual contributor roles", "Must include team management"]

CULTURE FIT (weight: 25%)
- Company stage: [YOUR PREFERENCE, e.g., "Series A-C, 50-500 employees"]
- Work style: [e.g., "Hybrid preferred, open to remote"]
- Values alignment: [e.g., "Mission-driven, data-informed culture, low bureaucracy"]
- Dealbreakers: [e.g., "No fully in-office 5 days/week"]

GROWTH TRAJECTORY (weight: 25%)
- Learning opportunities: [WHAT YOU WANT TO LEARN, e.g., "international expansion, enterprise sales"]
- Promotion path: [e.g., "Clear path to VP within 2-3 years"]
- Mentorship/leadership: [e.g., "Reports to C-suite, manages team of 3+"]
- Dealbreakers: [e.g., "No dead-end lateral moves"]

COMPENSATION ALIGNMENT (weight: 20%)
- Base salary range: [YOUR RANGE, e.g., "$150K-$190K"]
- Equity importance: [e.g., "Important — prefer meaningful equity at growth stage"]
- Benefits priorities: [e.g., "Health insurance, 401k match, flexible PTO"]
- Dealbreakers: [e.g., "Below $140K base"]

PART 3: ANALYSIS INSTRUCTIONS

For each job posting I share, provide:

1. MATCH SCORE (out of 100) with breakdown by dimension
2. TOP 3 REASONS this role fits (be specific to my criteria)
3. TOP 3 CONCERNS or gaps (be honest about mismatches)
4. MISSING INFORMATION — what's not in the posting that I should ask about
5. VERDICT: Strong Match / Worth Exploring / Likely Skip

After analyzing all postings, rank them from strongest to weakest match and explain your top recommendation in 2-3 sentences.

Example: Completed Prompt

Here is what the prompt looks like filled in for a marketing professional. Use this as a reference when completing your own.

Filled-In Example

PART 1: CONTEXT SETTING

I'm conducting a targeted job search and need your help analyzing job postings against my personal criteria. Here's my background:

Current/most recent role: Senior Marketing Manager at a B2B SaaS company (Series C, 200 employees)
Years of experience: 7
Key skills: demand generation, content strategy, marketing automation (HubSpot, Marketo), data analysis, paid media management, team leadership, cross-functional collaboration
Industry background: B2B SaaS, fintech, martech
Target role type: Director of Marketing or Head of Marketing at a growth-stage startup (Series A-C)

PART 2: MY ROLE-FIT SCORECARD

Rate each job against these criteria on a 1-5 scale:

FUNCTION FIT (weight: 30%)
- Role matches my target title and level: MUST-HAVE (Director or Head-level)
- Uses my core skills daily: demand generation, content strategy, marketing automation
- Growth trajectory aligns with my 3-year goal: VP of Marketing
- Dealbreakers: No pure individual contributor roles. Must include team management (3+ direct reports).

CULTURE FIT (weight: 25%)
- Company stage: Series A-C, 50-500 employees
- Work style: Hybrid preferred, open to fully remote
- Values alignment: Mission-driven, data-informed culture, low bureaucracy, fast decision-making
- Dealbreakers: No fully in-office 5 days/week. No companies with primarily outbound sales culture.

GROWTH TRAJECTORY (weight: 25%)
- Learning opportunities: International expansion, enterprise go-to-market, board-level reporting
- Promotion path: Clear path to VP Marketing within 2-3 years
- Mentorship/leadership: Reports to CEO or CRO, manages team of 3+
- Dealbreakers: No dead-end lateral moves. No reporting to a junior manager.

COMPENSATION ALIGNMENT (weight: 20%)
- Base salary range: $155K-$190K
- Equity importance: Important — prefer meaningful equity at growth stage (0.05%+)
- Benefits priorities: Health insurance, 401k match, flexible PTO, professional development budget
- Dealbreakers: Below $140K base. No equity offered.

Tips for Better Results

  • Start with 5-10 postings, not 50. Quality analysis beats batch volume. The AI gives better reasoning when it can deeply compare a manageable set.
  • Be specific in your dealbreakers. Vague criteria like "good culture" give vague results. Say exactly what you mean: "No fully in-office 5 days/week" or "Must manage 3+ people."
  • Update your scorecard weekly. After running the prompt a few times, you'll discover criteria you forgot. Add them. Remove ones that don't matter as much as you thought.
  • Compare the AI's scoring against your gut feeling. When they disagree, figure out why. You might be missing a criterion, or you might be weighting something incorrectly.
  • Save your completed prompt somewhere permanent. You'll reuse it every morning as part of your daily routine. Keep it in a note, doc, or text file you can access quickly.
