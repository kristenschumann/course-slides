Foundations

Place value review

Every digit in a decimal has a place value based on powers of 10. Consider 7,532.418:

Position Thousands Hundreds Tens Ones . Tenths Hundredths Thousandths
Digit 7 5 3 2 . 4 1 8
Value $10^3$ $10^2$ $10^1$ $10^0$ $10^{-1}$ $10^{-2}$ $10^{-3}$

Moving LEFT = multiply by 10 (bigger)  •  Moving RIGHT = divide by 10 (smaller)

Multiplying

Multiplying by powers of 10

Rule: When multiplying by $10^n$, move the decimal point n places to the RIGHT.

Expression Shift Result
$3.456 \times 10$ 1 place right $34.56$
$3.456 \times 100$ 2 places right $345.6$
$3.456 \times 1{,}000$ 3 places right $3{,}456$

Note: $10^0 = 1$, so multiplying by $10^0$ changes nothing.

Multiplying

Worked example

Problem

$0.0072 \times 10{,}000 = \;?$

Solution

$10{,}000 = 10^4$, so move 4 places right

$0.0072 \to 0.072 \to 0.72 \to 7.2 \to 72$

Answer: $72$

Negative Exponents

Multiplying by $10^{-n}$

Key insight: Multiplying by $10^{-n}$ is the same as dividing by $10^n$.

Example

$5.6 \times 10^{-3}$

$= 5.6 \div 1{,}000$

$=$ $0.0056$

Rule Summary

  • $\times 10^{-n}$ = move decimal left n places
  • $\div 10^{-n}$ = move decimal right n places
5:45 - 6:15 | Explain that negative exponents flip the direction. Multiplying by 10^-3 is the same as dividing by 1000. Move the decimal 3 places left.

Scientific Notation

The $a \times 10^n$ format

Format: $a \times 10^n$ where $1 \leq a < 10$

To Scientific Notation

Move the decimal until you have a number between 1 and 10

Count the number of places moved = exponent

From Scientific Notation

Positive exponent: shift decimal right

Negative exponent: shift decimal left

Key Idea

Scientific notation appears in data interpretation and word problems involving very large or very small numbers.

Quantitative comparison

Column A

$0.3 \times 0.3$

Column B

$0.09 \times 10$

Solution

Column A: $0.3 \times 0.3 = 0.09$

Column B: $0.09 \times 10 = 0.9$ (shift decimal 1 place right)

$0.09 < 0.9$, so answer is Column B is greater

GRE Practice

Scientific notation in word problems

Problem

The population of a city increased from $1.2 \times 10^6$ to $1.38 \times 10^6$. What was the percent increase?

Solution

Change: $1.38 \times 10^6 - 1.2 \times 10^6 = 0.18 \times 10^6$

Percent increase $= \frac{0.18 \times 10^6}{1.2 \times 10^6} \times 100$

The $10^6$ cancels: $\frac{0.18}{1.2} = 0.15$

Answer: $15\%$

9:00 - 9:45 | GRE-format problem solving. Click to reveal solution. The key insight is that when both numbers share the same power of 10, you can factor it out and work with the decimals. Change is 0.18 million. Divide by original 1.2 million. The powers of 10 cancel. 0.18 divided by 1.2 equals 0.15, which is 15%. This is a common GRE pattern in data interpretation.

Key Takeaways

Decimals & powers of ten

  • Multiply by $10^n$: Move decimal RIGHT n places
  • Divide by $10^n$: Move decimal LEFT n places
  • Negative exponents flip direction: $\times 10^{-n}$ moves LEFT, $\div 10^{-n}$ moves RIGHT
  • Decimal-to-fraction: Count decimal places for the power-of-10 denominator, then simplify
  • Scientific notation: Write as $a \times 10^n$ where $1 \leq a < 10$
  • Memory trick: Multiply = More = Right, Divide = Diminish = Left

This eliminates decimal errors and saves significant time.
