Understanding Waitlists

Why Schools Use Waitlists

Class Yield

Schools can't predict how many admits will accept. The waitlist fills remaining spots.

You're Competitive

Being waitlisted means they saw real potential. Otherwise they would have rejected you.

Odds Vary Widely

Some schools pull heavily from waitlists, others rarely. Research your specific school's history.

1:00 - 2:15 | Why do schools waitlist? They can't perfectly predict yield, so they use waitlists to fill the class. Being waitlisted means you were competitive. Research your school's history to calibrate expectations.

Continued Interest Letter

What a Strong Letter Looks Like

Reference specific post-application engagement and connect it back to your career goals. Demonstrate your interest is grounded in real research, not just enthusiasm.

Keep it to 2-3 paragraphs. Mention a specific event you attended, a student you spoke with, or a program element that deepened your conviction about the school.

3:30 - 5:00 | Your continued interest letter should be short and specific. Reference something you've done since applying. Maybe you attended an event, spoke with a current student, or learned about a specific program. Connect that back to your career goals. Show them your interest is grounded in research, not just generic enthusiasm. Keep it to 2-3 paragraphs maximum.

Ongoing Communication

Share Meaningful Updates Only

Worth sharing
  • Promotion or new leadership role
  • Improved GMAT/GRE score
  • Major project completion or award
  • Genuine engagement with the school
Skip these
  • Routine performance reviews
  • "I read an article about your school"
  • Multiple notes without new info
  • Emotional appeals or desperation

Contact every 4-6 weeks if you have genuine updates. Silence is better than meaningless noise.

5:00 - 6:30 | After your initial letter, only share updates that would actually change the committee's evaluation. A promotion, yes. An improved test score, yes. A major project or award, yes. But a routine performance review, no. Reading an article about the school, no. Emotional appeals, absolutely not. Contact every 4-6 weeks if you have real news. Silence is always better than noise.

Update Quality

The Meaningful Update Test

  • Ask yourself: "Would this change the committee's evaluation of my candidacy?"
  • The bar: New information that strengthens a specific dimension of your profile.
  • Format: Brief professional emails referencing your continued interest with the specific update.
6:30 - 7:30 | Before sending any update, apply this test: would this change the committee's evaluation? The bar for meaningful is new information that strengthens a specific dimension of your candidacy. A promotion from Associate to Senior Associate, yes. Completing a relevant certification, yes. Attending a conference, probably not unless something significant happened. Format should be brief professional emails. Reference your continued interest, share the update, close graciously.

Multiple Decisions

Navigating Competing Deadlines

Deposit at Your Backup

Put down a deposit at an admitted school. This is normal and expected. Schools know some deposits get withdrawn.

Ask About Timeline

Email admissions asking for guidance on waitlist timing, especially if you have deposit deadlines elsewhere.

Be Honest

If asked about other waitlists, be truthful. If admitted off a waitlist after depositing, withdraw respectfully and promptly.

Protect Yourself

Don't turn down solid admits hoping for a waitlist miracle. Have a backup you'd be happy attending.

7:30 - 9:00 | Waitlists create complicated decisions. If you have an admit elsewhere, deposit at your backup. This is normal. Schools expect it. Ask the waitlist school if they can share timing guidance, especially if you have deadlines at other schools. Be honest throughout the process. And most importantly, protect yourself. Never turn down a solid admit hoping for a waitlist miracle. Always have a backup you'd be happy attending.

Have another admit Commit fully to your admitted school. Many students thrive at their "second choice" school.
No admits this cycle Evaluate whether to reapply next cycle with improvements to your profile.
9:00 - 10:15 | When the decision comes, be ready. If admitted off the waitlist, respond quickly. Deadlines are tight, and the school may move to the next person. Withdraw from other schools promptly and professionally. If not admitted, remember it's not a failure. Sometimes the math doesn't work out. If this school is truly your goal, consider reapplying next year. If you have another admit, commit fully to it. And if you have no admits this cycle, evaluate whether to strengthen your profile and reapply.
