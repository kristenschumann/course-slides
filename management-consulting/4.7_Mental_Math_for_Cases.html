Label Arithmetic

Strip the Zeros

Write 5 x 3M = 15M, not 5 x 3,000,000. Never write out all the zeros.

Label Rules

K x K = M

M x K = B

B / M = K

1:15 - 1:45 | Label arithmetic eliminates the most common case math error. Key points: - Zero errors are the #1 killer in case math - Use K, M, B labels instead of writing full numbers - K x K = M, M x K = B: memorize these rules

  • Divide by 5: multiply by 2, then divide by 10. 730 / 5 = 1,460 / 10 = 146
  • Divide by 25: multiply by 4, then divide by 100. 3,200 / 25 = 12,800 / 100 = 128

The Rule of 72

Divide 72 by the growth rate to find the doubling time. At 8% growth, assets double in about 9 years.

It works in reverse too. Revenue doubled in 6 years? CAGR is roughly 72 / 6 = 12%. Accurate enough for any case interview.

2:50 - 3:15 | The Rule of 72 handles compound growth quickly. Key points: - 72 / growth rate = doubling time - Works in reverse: 72 / years to double = implied growth rate - Accurate enough for case interview purposes

Key Takeaways

Mental Math for Cases

  • Math errors are the #1 rejection reason. Speed matters, but accuracy matters more.
  • Use K, M, B labels. Never write out full numbers. This eliminates the most common arithmetic mistake.
  • Convert percentages to fractions. Dividing by 4 is always faster than multiplying by 0.25 in your head.
  • Never calculate silently. Structure, narrate, and sanity-check every calculation out loud.
  • 25 minutes a day for 20 sessions. That is all it takes to build reliable speed.
