Signal Words and
Sentence Direction

0:00 - 0:30 | Hook: Here's a trick that will immediately improve your TC and SE scores: pay attention to signal words. They tell you which direction the sentence is going, and once you know the direction, predicting the answer becomes much easier.

Continuation

Same-direction signals

These words signal continuation: the sentence keeps going in the same direction.

Signal Word What It Does
and, moreover, furthermore Adds similar or supporting information
similarly, likewise Shows parallel ideas
indeed, in fact Confirms or emphasizes
therefore, thus, so Shows conclusion or result
Example

"The research was thorough and _______, leaving no aspect unexamined." "And" = same direction. Predict: comprehensive.

0:30 - 2:30 | Same-direction signals mean the blank will have a similar meaning or tone to what came before. Key words: and, moreover, furthermore, similarly, likewise, indeed, in fact, therefore, thus, so. Also the colon often signals explanation or restatement. Example shows how "and" tells us the blank matches "thorough."

The Shift

Contrast signals

These words signal a shift: the sentence is about to go in a different direction.

Signal Word What It Does
but, however, yet Introduces direct contrast
although, even though Introduces concession
despite, in spite of Introduces unexpected contrast
nevertheless, whereas, while Shows contrast or comparison
Watch Out

"While" can mean "during the time that" OR "whereas." Read carefully to determine which applies.

2:30 - 5:00 | Contrast signals mean the blank has the opposite meaning. Key words: but, however, yet, although, even though, despite, nevertheless, whereas, while. Example: "Although the speaker's manner was confident, his arguments were actually quite _______." Although = contrast, so predict opposite of confident: weak, flimsy, unconvincing.

Logical Links

Cause and effect signals

Cause Signals

  • because
  • since
  • due to

These explain WHY something happened.

Effect Signals

  • therefore, thus
  • consequently, as a result
  • for this reason

These show WHAT resulted from the cause.

Example

"Because the instructions were _______, many customers returned the product." The effect (returned) tells us the cause is negative: confusing, incomprehensible.

5:00 - 6:30 | Cause and effect signals show that one thing leads to or explains another. Cause signals: because, since, due to. Effect signals: therefore, thus, consequently, as a result. Use the known part (cause or effect) to predict the unknown part. Also watch for punctuation doing signal work: colons and dashes often introduce explanation or parallel structure without an explicit signal word.

Degree Markers

Intensifiers and qualifiers

Intensifiers

Word Effect
even More extreme than expected
especially Emphasizes degree
remarkably Signals unusual degree

Qualifiers

Word Effect
somewhat Reduces intensity
rather Moderate degree
fairly Moderate degree
Example

"The weather was bad, but the final day was even more _______." "Even" signals worse than "bad": miserable, disastrous.

6:30 - 8:00 | Intensifiers (even, especially, remarkably, extremely) signal that something is more extreme than expected. Qualifiers (somewhat, rather, fairly) reduce intensity. "Even" is particularly important because it tells you the blank is a stronger version of what came before. These words help you calibrate the degree of your prediction.

Putting It Together

Complete walkthrough

"While critics _______ the novel for its experimental structure, the general public embraced it enthusiastically."

Step 1: Identify signal

"While" signals contrast. Note "embraced enthusiastically" as the clue.

Step 2: Determine direction

Public loved it. "While" means critics did the opposite.

Step 3: Predict

Critics must have criticized, condemned, or dismissed it.

Step 4: Match

(A) lauded (B) derided (C) ignored (D) celebrated (E) endorsed

8:00 - 9:00 | Complete walkthrough: "While" signals contrast with the public embracing the novel. Critics did the opposite. Predict: criticized, condemned. Match to choices: lauded, celebrated, endorsed are positive (wrong). Ignored is neutral. Derided means mocked/criticized, which is the perfect contrast. Answer is B.

Avoid These Mistakes

Common signal word traps

Misreading Contrast

Reading "although" or "while" as "because." These signal contrast, not cause. Don't let the structure confuse you.

Double Negatives

"Not unlike" = similar. "Fail to disappoint" = satisfy. Two negatives flip the direction back to positive.

Late Signals

Sometimes the key signal comes AFTER the blank: "The speaker was _______, yet the audience remained engaged."

Remember

Always read the complete sentence before predicting. The signal word might be anywhere, not just before the blank.

9:00 - 9:30 | Three traps to watch for: misreading contrast words as cause words, double negatives that flip direction back ("not unlike" = similar), and signals that come after the blank rather than before. Always read the full sentence before making your prediction.

Practice

Spot the signal word

Despite her reputation for being _______, the professor was surprisingly approachable and happy to answer students' questions during office hours.

  1. accessible
  2. gregarious
  3. aloof
  4. cordial
  5. affable
~1 min | Read the question aloud. Give viewers time to identify the signal word and predict an answer before moving on. Prompt: "What signal word do you see? What direction does it set up?"

Practice

Answer walkthrough

Answer

(C) aloof — distant and unapproachable

Identify the signal

"Despite" is a contrast signal word. It tells you the blank must oppose what follows.

Determine direction

The professor IS approachable. "Despite" means her reputation is the OPPOSITE.

Predict and match

Predict: distant, cold, unfriendly. Aloof (distant/unapproachable) is the perfect match.

Eliminate wrong answers

Accessible, gregarious, cordial, and affable are all positive traits — they match "approachable," not contrast it.

~1 min | Walk through step by step. "Despite" is a contrast signal from Slide 3. The professor is approachable, so her reputation must be the opposite. Aloof means distant — perfect contrast. The other four choices are all positive/friendly, which would match "approachable" rather than oppose it. This is exactly the technique from this lesson.

Key Takeaways

Signal words and sentence direction

  • Same-direction signals (and, moreover, indeed, therefore) mean the blank matches the clue
  • Contrast signals (but, although, despite, yet) mean the blank is the opposite of the clue
  • Cause/effect signals (because, therefore, consequently) link reasons to outcomes. Use the known part to predict the unknown
  • Intensifiers and qualifiers (even, somewhat) tell you the degree of the word you need
  • Read the full sentence first, identify signals, determine direction, predict, then match
9:30 - 10:00 | Summary: Same-direction signals mean the blank matches the clue. Contrast signals mean the opposite. Cause/effect links reasons to outcomes. Intensifiers tell you degree. Always read the full sentence, identify signals, determine direction, predict, then match. Master this and you'll answer TC and SE questions faster and more accurately.
