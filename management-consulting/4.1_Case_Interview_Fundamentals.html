The Case Interview

A 30-40 minute structured business problem where the interviewer presents a real client scenario and evaluates how you think through it, not whether you get the "right answer."

Cases are drawn from real consulting engagements. At BCG, interviewers develop cases from their own projects. At McKinsey, a central team creates cases with scripted question flows.

Three Firms, Three Formats

How McKinsey, BCG, and Bain each run cases differently

1:45 | Section transition. Preparing for McKinsey is tactically different from preparing for Bain.

Break Into Management Consulting

MBB Case Formats

McKinsey

Interviewer-led. Centrally developed cases with scripted questions. You handle each question as a standalone mini-problem.

BCG

Hybrid format. Cases from the interviewer's own projects. You drive direction, but they redirect and challenge. Values creativity.

Bain

Candidate-led. You propose areas to investigate and analyses to run. Tests prioritization and strategic judgment.

1:45 - 3:15 | McKinsey: interviewer-led, centrally developed, scripted. You react to direction. BCG: hybrid, cases from own projects, values creativity and original thinking. Bain: candidate-led, you decide where to focus, tests prioritization. Bain also uses written cases and SOVA in some offices.

The Key Distinction

Interviewer-Led (McKinsey)

  • They tell you what to analyze next
  • Tests how you handle new constraints
  • You react to direction and pivot quickly

Candidate-Led (BCG/Bain)

  • You decide what to explore
  • Tests your prioritization and judgment
  • Demands confidence in your own direction
3:15 - 3:45 | Core distinction: interviewer-led means reacting to direction, candidate-led means creating direction. Both require structuring, but candidate-led demands higher confidence. If applying to multiple firms, practice both styles.

How You're Actually Scored

Five dimensions, weighted by importance

3:45 | Section transition. Most candidates go in without knowing the grading criteria.

Break Into Management Consulting

Five Scoring Dimensions

  • Structured Thinking (highest weight). Break problems into clear, logical parts.
  • Analytical Problem-Solving (highest weight). Math, data interpretation, insights.
  • Business Acumen. Commercial judgment and market awareness.
  • Communication. Clear, top-down delivery under pressure.
  • Cultural Fit. Interpersonal skills and team compatibility.
3:45 - 4:45 | Five dimensions scored 1-5. Structured thinking and problem-solving carry the most weight. Then communication. Then business judgment and interpersonal skills.

Break Into Management Consulting

What Separates Top Scores

You cannot score a 5 without demonstrating confidence and enthusiasm alongside strong mechanics. A flat, robotic delivery caps your score even if the analysis is perfect.

A candidate with all 4s outperforms someone with one 5 and multiple 3s. Firms prefer consistency. Uneven performance raises concerns about client readiness.

4:45 - 5:30 | Confidence and enthusiasm required for top scores. Consistency over spikes. All 4s beats one 5 with multiple 3s. Focus on eliminating weaknesses rather than being brilliant in one area.

Break Into Management Consulting

Opening and Structure

Phase 1: Opening

Listen carefully to the full prompt. Restate in your own words. Confirm the primary objective. Ask 2-3 clarifying questions that narrow the scope.

Phase 2: Structure

Take 60-90 seconds of silence to think. Build a MECE issue tree tailored to this case. Present top-down. State an initial hypothesis.

The structure phase is where most cases are won or lost. A weak structure means analyzing the wrong things for the next 25 minutes.

5:45 - 7:00 | Phase 1: Don't rush. Pause, confirm you understand, ask questions that narrow scope. Phase 2: The most important phase. Take silence to build structure. MECE, top-down, state a hypothesis. Weak structure undermines everything.

Break Into Management Consulting

Analysis and Recommendation

Phase 3: Analysis

Work through your structure with data. Interpret charts, perform calculations. Connect findings to your hypothesis. Explain why insights matter, not just what the data shows.

Phase 4: Recommendation

Lead with the answer. Support with 2-3 reasons from your analysis. Acknowledge key risks. Suggest concrete next steps. Top-down, not bottom-up.

Great candidates go beyond observation to insight. They don't just report what the data shows; they explain why it matters and what to do about it.

7:00 - 8:00 | Phase 3: 15-25 minutes of working through data. The insight gap separates good from great. Phase 4: CEO elevator pitch. Answer first, evidence second. Acknowledge risks, suggest next steps. This is how consultants communicate.

Key Takeaways

Case Interview Fundamentals

  • It's an audition, not a test. The interviewer evaluates how you think, communicate, and solve problems, not whether you reach the right answer.
  • Three firms, three formats. McKinsey is interviewer-led, BCG is hybrid, Bain is candidate-led. Know the format and practice both styles.
  • Five scoring dimensions. Structured thinking, analytical problem-solving, business acumen, communication, and cultural fit. Consistency beats brilliance with gaps.
  • Four phases every time. Opening, structure, analysis, recommendation. The structure phase is where most cases are won or lost.
  • Plan for 25-30 practice cases. Start with structure drills, move to live cases, and consider coaching to accelerate your progress.
9:45 - 10:00 | Five key takeaways. Audition mindset. Know the firm formats. Five scoring dimensions with consistency. Four phases with structure as the linchpin. 25-30 practice cases as the benchmark.
