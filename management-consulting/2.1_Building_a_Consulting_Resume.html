What Recruiters Scan For

  • Academic achievement: School name, GPA, honors, and analytical major
  • Impact-driven experience: Recognizable companies and quantified results
  • Quantified results: Numbers, action verbs, and specific outcomes in every bullet

Format and Structure

The non-negotiable formatting rules for consulting resumes

1:30 - 1:40 | Transition to format and structure requirements.

Management Consulting Course

The One-Page Rule

One page. Always. Even with 10+ years of experience, your consulting resume fits on one page. PDF format, clean minimalist design, no colors or graphics.

Consulting values concision. If you cannot distill your career into one page, you are signaling that you cannot prioritize what matters.

1:40 - 2:15 | One-page rule is universal. PDF format, no exceptions. Clean minimalist design signals consulting mindset. Every slide deck you build for clients will require ruthless editing. Your resume is the first test of that skill.

Management Consulting Course

Essential Formatting Rules

  • Single clean font: Times New Roman or Arial, 10-12pt body
  • Margins no narrower than 10mm on all sides
  • PDF format for every submission
  • Consistent date formatting throughout (pick one style)
  • Reverse chronological order in every section
2:15 - 2:45 | Body text 10-12pt, headers 14-16pt. No colors, no graphics. Consistent date formatting is a common trap. If you write "December 2024" in one section, don't switch to "12/2024" in another.

Resume Section Order

Undergrad / Recent Grad

  • Education (lead with school + GPA)
  • Professional Experience

Experienced Hire (1+ years)

2:45 - 3:00 | Section order depends on career stage. Undergrads lead with education because school and GPA are strongest credentials. Experienced hires flip it and lead with work experience.
Part 3

The Action-Result Bullet Formula

The single most important skill for your consulting resume

3:00 - 3:10 | Transition to the bullet formula. This is the core skill that separates good resumes from great ones.

Management Consulting Course

The Bullet Formula

Action

Start with a strong past-tense verb. The recruiter identifies your skill from the first word.

Context

Describe what you did and for whom. Make your achievement real and specific.

Result

Close with a quantified outcome. Revenue, cost savings, efficiency gains, or scope.

3:10 - 3:45 | Every bullet follows Action + Context + Measurable Result. Start with a verb like analyzed, designed, implemented, led, delivered. Add context: what you did, for whom. Close with a number. Non-negotiable.

Management Consulting Course

Bullet Comparison: Specificity

Weak

Vague achievement with no context

"Improved process efficiency by 40%."

Strong

Specific action, method, and result

"Designed an operational efficiency framework that streamlined workflow automation, reducing processing time by 20%."

3:45 - 4:15 | Weak: the number exists but the bullet is hollow. What process? What did you do? Strong: now I know what you built, how you built it, and the outcome.

Management Consulting Course

Bullet Comparison: Impact

Weak

Activity description, no outcome

"Worked on strategic framework for client."

Strong

Specific action, client context, dollar result

"Developed and implemented a cost-reduction strategy for a logistics client, resulting in $250K in annual savings."

4:15 - 4:45 | Weak: sounds like consulting, means nothing. Strong: specific action, specific client context, dollar-value result. That is what gets you noticed.
Part 4

Quantification Strategies

What to do when you don't have revenue numbers

5:00 - 5:10 | Transition. Not every role produces revenue or cost savings. But you still need numbers.

Management Consulting Course

Two Ways to Quantify Impact

Business Metrics

Revenue growth, cost savings, market share gains, customers acquired, efficiency improvements.

Scope Metrics

Team size led, stakeholders managed, reports produced, events organized, budget managed.

5:10 - 5:45 | Preferred metrics: revenue, cost, market share, efficiency. If you don't have those, quantify scope. "Led a team of 12 volunteers to organize a 300-person fundraising event" is better than "Organized events for nonprofit."

Management Consulting Course

Clarify the Baseline

Context matters. A 40% increase from $1,000 is $400. A 40% increase from $1M is $400,000. If the baseline is small, use absolute numbers instead of percentages.

Be honest about the scale of your impact. Recruiters will ask follow-up questions in interviews.

5:45 - 6:15 | Always clarify the baseline. 40% sounds impressive but context matters. If the baseline is small, use absolute numbers. Be honest about scale.

Management Consulting Course

Choose Action Verbs Strategically

Results / Achievement

Delivered, Achieved, Exceeded, Increased, Reduced, Generated

Analysis / Strategy

Analyzed, Evaluated, Designed, Formulated, Assessed, Identified

Leadership

Led, Managed, Implemented, Launched, Mentored, Directed

Communication

Presented, Negotiated, Advised, Collaborated, Partnered, Articulated

6:15 - 7:00 | The verb signals your skill set. Results verbs for achievement. Analytical verbs for strategy work. Leadership verbs for management. Communication verbs for collaboration. Vary verbs across bullets to signal breadth.

Management Consulting Course

The GPA Decision

There is no official GPA cutoff at MBB, but 3.5 or higher is considered safe. A 3.6+ insures against any academic questions. Context matters: a 3.3 in engineering is stronger than a 3.7 in communications.

3.5+ always include. 3.2-3.5 judgment call, compensate with strong achievements. Below 3.2 consider omitting and let your experience carry the weight.

7:00 - 7:30 | No official cutoff but 3.5+ is safe. Analytical majors treated favorably. GPA gets you on the interview list, case performance gets you the offer.

Management Consulting Course

What Kills Resumes

  • Generic language: Vague bullets like "responsible for managing" signal vague thinking
  • No quantification: Every bullet needs a number, no exceptions
  • Untranslated jargon: Company acronyms and insider terms get skipped
  • Team language: "We implemented" is weak. Take credit for your contribution
  • Inconsistent formatting: Mixed date styles, bullet types, or fonts
  • More than one page: Two pages signals you cannot edit
7:30 - 8:30 | Six common resume killers. Generic language, missing numbers, jargon, team framing, inconsistent formatting, and going beyond one page. Each of these gets your resume thrown in the "no" pile.

Key Takeaways

Building Your Consulting Resume

  • Survive the 7-second scan. Clean formatting, quantified results, no vague language. Structure your resume for a skim, not a deep read.
  • One page, always. PDF format, consistent fonts and dates, margins no narrower than 10mm. Ruthless editing is the first test of consulting skill.
  • Every bullet: Action + Context + Result. Start with a strong verb, describe what you did, end with a number.
  • MBB screeners look for three traits: inclusive leadership, entrepreneurial drive, and personal impact.
  • Your resume gets you the interview. Your case performance gets you the offer. Get the resume right, then move on to cases and fit.
9:00 - 10:00 | Five key takeaways. Resume is the gatekeeper. One page. Action-Context-Result formula. Demonstrate leadership, drive, and impact. Resume gets you the interview, cases get you the offer.
