Watch Out

Common DI Traps (1-3)

Trap 1

Wrong Units

Graph says "in thousands" but you answer in actual numbers.

Trap 2

Wrong Base

"Percent greater than" is not the same as "percent of."

Trap 3

Visual Deception

3D graphs and broken axes distort proportions.

4:00-4:45 | Walk through traps 1-3. Trap 1: always check the unit multiplier in axis labels. Trap 2: percent greater than vs percent of is a classic GRE distinction. Trap 3: always verify visual impressions with actual numbers.

Watch Out

Common DI Traps (4-5)

Trap 4

Wrong Source

Using the wrong graph or mismatching years between displays.

Trap 5

Careless Reading

"Revenue" vs "Profit," "Total" vs "Average," "2018-2020" vs "from 2018 to 2020."

4:45-5:30 | Traps 4-5. Trap 4: with multiple displays, confirm you are reading the right one. Trap 5: read every word carefully.

Part 2

Approximation vs. Precision

When to estimate and when to calculate exactly

5:30-5:40 | Transition: Knowing when to approximate is just as important as knowing how to calculate.

Efficiency

Approximate or Be Precise?

Approximate
  • Answer choices are spread apart (25%, 40%, 55%)
  • Question says "approximately"
  • You are running low on time
Be Precise
  • Answer choices are close (23%, 25%, 27%)
  • Question asks for "exactly"
  • Small table with exact numbers
Quick Check

Look at the answer choices first. The spacing between them tells you how precise you need to be.

5:40-7:00 | Explain the decision framework. Approximate when answer choices are spread apart or the question uses "approximately." Be precise when choices are close together. Always check the answer spacing first.

Techniques

Approximation Shortcuts

Rounding

Round messy numbers to nearby clean values.

47% $\approx$ 50%

\$2,847,000 $\approx$ \$2.8 million

Benchmarks

Use fraction-percent equivalents you know.

$\frac{1}{3} \approx 33\%$,   $\frac{1}{4} = 25\%$

$\frac{3}{4} = 75\%$,   $\frac{1}{5} = 20\%$

Example

What is 19% of 52? Approximate: 20% of 50 = 10. Actual: 9.88. Close enough.

7:00-8:00 | Two main approximation shortcuts. Rounding: turn messy numbers into nearby clean ones. Benchmarks: use the fraction-percent equivalents you already know. Show the 19% of 52 example to prove approximation works.

Time Management

Budget Your DI Time

Skip Signal

If you have spent 2+ minutes without progress, mark it and move on. Return with fresh eyes.

Summary

How to Approach DI Questions

  • Scan first -- Spend 30 seconds on title, labels, units, and scale before reading questions
  • Read precisely -- Identify exactly what is being asked and which data source to use
  • Calculate last -- Extract specific values, compute, and verify your answer makes sense
  • Check answer spacing -- Spread-apart choices allow approximation; close choices demand precision
  • Watch for traps -- Verify units, comparison bases, and graph scales before finalizing
  • Manage your time -- Answer easy questions first, skip and return to hard ones
9:30-10:00 | Summary: The three-step approach is scan, read, calculate. Check answer spacing to decide precision. Watch for the five common traps. Manage your time by tackling easy questions first.
