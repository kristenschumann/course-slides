Session 3 Resource

LinkedIn Profile Audit Prompt

A comprehensive AI-powered audit of your LinkedIn profile plus a 15-item checklist to ensure your profile is optimized for recruiter searches.

Headline Formula

Your headline is the most important line on your profile. It appears in search results, connection requests, and every comment you leave. Go beyond your job title.

[Target Role] | [Value Proposition] | [Proof Point]
  • Marketing Director | Scaling B2B SaaS from $5M to $50M ARR | Ex-HubSpot
  • Data Scientist | Turning messy data into revenue decisions | Python, SQL, ML
  • Product Manager | 0→1 products in fintech | 3 launches, 2M+ users

About Section Structure

Your About section is your pitch. Structure it in four parts so readers understand who you are, what you do, and how to connect.

Hook

1 sentence

Open with a surprising stat, bold claim, or compelling question that makes the reader want more.

Story

2-3 sentences

Your career arc -- where you started, what you've accomplished, and the thread that ties it together.

Skills

2-3 sentences

What you're known for, your superpowers, the tools and approaches that set you apart.

CTA

1 sentence

What you're looking for and how to reach you. Make it easy for the right people to take action.

The Audit Prompt

Copy this prompt into ChatGPT or Claude. Replace the bracketed sections with your actual LinkedIn profile information, then review the scored audit and rewrite suggestions.

Perform a comprehensive audit of my LinkedIn profile. I'll share my profile details below.

MY LINKEDIN PROFILE:
Headline: [YOUR CURRENT HEADLINE]
About: [YOUR CURRENT ABOUT SECTION]
Experience (most recent 3 roles):
- [ROLE 1: Title, Company, Duration, Description]
- [ROLE 2: Title, Company, Duration, Description]
- [ROLE 3: Title, Company, Duration, Description]
Skills (top 10): [LIST THEM]
Target role: [WHAT YOU'RE LOOKING FOR]

AUDIT CHECKLIST — Score each item 1-5 and provide specific recommendations:

HEADLINE (3 items):
1. Does it go beyond just a job title?
2. Does it include keywords recruiters search for?
3. Does it communicate unique value?

ABOUT SECTION (4 items):
4. Does the first line hook the reader?
5. Is there a clear career narrative (not just a list)?
6. Are relevant keywords naturally incorporated?
7. Does it end with a call to action?

EXPERIENCE (4 items):
8. Do bullets start with strong action verbs?
9. Are results quantified (numbers, percentages, dollar amounts)?
10. Is the most relevant experience detailed enough?
11. Are keywords from target roles incorporated?

SKILLS & EXTRAS (4 items):
12. Are top 3 skills aligned with target role?
13. Is the Featured section used effectively?
14. Is the profile photo professional and current?
15. Are there at least 2 recommendations?

For each item scoring below 4, provide a specific rewrite or action step.
End with your overall score (/75) and the top 3 changes that would have the biggest impact.

15-Item Audit Checklist

Use this visual checklist to track your progress. Work through each category and check off items as you optimize them.

Headline (3 items)
  • 1 Goes beyond just a job title -- includes a value proposition or differentiator
  • 2 Includes keywords that recruiters actually search for in your field
  • 3 Communicates unique value -- not generic or interchangeable with anyone else
About Section (4 items)
  • 4 First line hooks the reader with a bold claim, stat, or question
  • 5 Tells a clear career narrative, not just a list of responsibilities
  • 6 Relevant keywords are naturally woven in (not keyword-stuffed)
  • 7 Ends with a clear call to action -- what you want and how to reach you
Experience (4 items)
  • 8 Bullet points start with strong action verbs (led, built, launched, reduced)
  • 9 Results are quantified with numbers, percentages, or dollar amounts
  • 10 Most relevant experience is detailed enough to tell the full story
  • 11 Keywords from target job descriptions are incorporated into descriptions
Skills & Extras (4 items)
  • 12 Top 3 pinned skills are aligned with your target role
  • 13 Featured section showcases relevant work, articles, or media
  • 14 Profile photo is professional, current, and high-resolution
  • 15 At least 2 written recommendations from colleagues or managers

LinkedIn Optimization Tips

  • Turn on "Open to Work" privately. This signals to recruiters without broadcasting to your current employer. Go to your profile, click "Open to," and select "Recruiters only."
  • Customize your public profile URL. Change it from the default string of numbers to linkedin.com/in/yourname. It looks more professional and is easier to share on resumes.
  • Refresh your profile weekly. LinkedIn's algorithm favors recently updated profiles. Even small edits -- adding a skill, tweaking a bullet -- boost your visibility in recruiter searches.
  • Get strategic endorsements. Ask 3-5 colleagues to endorse your top skills. Profiles with endorsed skills appear in 2x more searches, according to LinkedIn's own data.
