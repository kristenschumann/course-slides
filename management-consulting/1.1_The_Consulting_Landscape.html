Break Into Management Consulting

Perception vs. Reality

The Perception

  • Glamorous strategy work
  • Corner offices and boardrooms
  • Flying first class

The Reality

  • PowerPoint decks at midnight
  • Excel models and data cleaning
  • Client politics and hotel loyalty points
1:30 - 2:30 | The day-to-day rhythm: Monday morning, fly to the client. Tuesday through Thursday, on-site with team meetings, client workshops, building slides, stress-testing Excel models. Friday, fly home and debrief. Your deliverables are PowerPoint decks and Excel models. The slide is the output, but the analysis is the product. That thinking is what clients pay $500,000 a month for.

The Firm Tiers

Understanding the consulting hierarchy

2:30 - 2:45 | Section break. Not all consulting firms are the same, and understanding the hierarchy will shape your entire recruiting strategy.

Break Into Management Consulting

Three Major Firm Tiers

MBB

McKinsey, BCG, Bain. Highest prestige, best exit opportunities, most selective with roughly a 5% offer rate.

Tier 2

Oliver Wyman, LEK, Kearney, Roland Berger, Accenture Strategy. Specialized focus with faster responsibility.

Big Four

Deloitte S&O, PwC Strategy&, EY-Parthenon, KPMG. Broader service lines, more levels, slower promotion.

2:45 - 4:15 | MBB is McKinsey, BCG, and Bain. Highest prestige, strongest exit opportunities into PE and corporate strategy. McKinsey alone receives around 60,000 applications for roughly 3,000 positions. Tier 2 firms like Oliver Wyman, LEK, and Kearney do excellent work. They offer faster responsibility and deeper specialization, but weaker brand recognition outside consulting. Big Four consulting arms have built strategy practices that compete with Tier 2 and occasionally MBB, but sit inside massive audit and tax organizations.

Break Into Management Consulting

Boutique Firms

Boutique firms specialize in a single domain: healthcare strategy, social impact, or tech advisory. Small teams, deep work, and you become a domain expert fast.

Note: Apply to MBB, plus 3-4 Tier 2 and Big Four firms where the work interests you. Boutiques are worth it if you have a clear industry passion. A spread gives you options, and options give you negotiating power.

4:15 - 5:00 | Boutique firms specialize in healthcare, social impact, tech advisory. Small teams, deep work, you become a domain expert fast. The trade-off: outside their niche, nobody knows who they are. My advice to every candidate: apply to MBB, also apply to 3-4 Tier 2 and Big Four firms where the work interests you. Boutiques are worth it if you have a clear industry passion. A spread gives you options, and options give you negotiating power.

Where consultants specialize

Major Practice Areas

Practice Area What It Covers Market Share / Trend
Strategy Market entry, M&A, restructuring Smallest by headcount, highest prestige
Operations Supply chain, procurement, manufacturing 29.15% of global market
Technology / Digital AI, digital transformation Fastest-growing, 40% of new engagements
Financial Advisory Due diligence, valuation, restructuring Steady demand, PE-driven
HR / Organizational Workforce transformation, change mgmt Growing with digital disruption
5:10 - 6:30 | Strategy is the marquee practice: CEO-level questions about market entry, acquisitions, restructuring. Operations is the largest at 29.15% of the global consulting market. Technology and digital is the fastest-growing area, with AI and digital transformation driving up to 40% of new engagements. Financial advisory covers due diligence and valuation. HR and organizational consulting handles workforce transformation. At MBB, most entry-level hires are generalists for the first two years. At Tier 2 and Big Four, you may specialize earlier.

Why This Matters

From knowledge to recruiting advantage

6:30 - 6:40 | Section break. Transition to why industry knowledge directly impacts recruiting success.

Break Into Management Consulting

Your Recruiting Advantage

  • Target the right firms for your background and goals
  • Give specific answers to "Why consulting?" and "Why this firm?"
  • Build a recruiting strategy, not just an application list
6:40 - 7:30 | Every conversation during recruiting tests whether you actually understand this industry. When a McKinsey interviewer asks "Why McKinsey and not BCG?", you need a real answer. When a Kearney partner asks "Why a Tier 2 firm?", you need something specific about their operations practice. Most candidates treat firm selection like a lottery. The candidates who get multiple offers treat it like a strategy project. They know which firms match their interests, which practice areas are growing, and where their background gives them an edge.

Key Takeaways

What to Remember

  • $358B industry built on solving problems companies can't solve themselves. The work is slides and spreadsheets; the value is the thinking.
  • Firm tiers matter. MBB opens the most doors. Tier 2 offers specialization. Big Four gives breadth. Boutiques give depth.
  • Digital and AI is the fastest-growing area, driving 40% of new engagements. Tech backgrounds have a competitive advantage.
  • Industry knowledge is the foundation for every "Why consulting?" and "Why this firm?" answer you will give.
7:30 - 8:00 | Four things to lock in. One: consulting is a $358 billion industry built on one value proposition, solving problems that companies can't solve themselves. Two: firm tiers are real and they matter. Know the trade-offs before you build your target list. Three: the fastest-growing area is digital and AI, now driving 40% of new engagements. If you have a tech background, that is your competitive advantage. Four: understanding the industry is the foundation for every "Why consulting?" and "Why this firm?" answer you will give.
