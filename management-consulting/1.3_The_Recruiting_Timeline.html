Three Recruiting Paths

Undergrad

On-campus recruiting drives everything. Internship apps open Jun-Sep; full-time apps Sep-Nov of senior year.

MBA

Career services runs the show. Networking starts Aug of year one; internship apps Nov-Dec; interviews Jan-Feb.

Experienced Hire

No fixed calendar. Rolling deadlines year-round. Hiring peaks in Q1 and Q3, aligned with budget cycles.

0:20 - 1:40 | There is no single consulting recruiting timeline. There are three, and they look completely different. Undergrads are driven by on-campus recruiting with non-negotiable dates. MBAs have the most structured and compressed timeline, with career services running the show. Experienced hires face rolling deadlines year-round, which sounds like a luxury but is actually a trap without self-imposed structure.

The Six Recruiting Phases

01

Research

4-6 months before apps. Figure out which firms, practice areas, and whether consulting is right for you.

02

Network

4 months before apps. Coffee chats, cold emails, and building relationships for referrals.

03

App Prep

2 months before deadlines. Resume optimization, cover letters, and expert reviews.

04

Apply

Sequence strategically. Second-tier firms first for practice, then dream firms when you're strongest.

05

Interview

2-6 weeks after apps close. First round: 2 cases + fit. Final round: 3-4 interviews over a half day.

06

Offer

1-2 weeks after final round. You'll typically get 2-4 weeks to decide on your offer.

3:00 - 4:00 | Whatever your path, the phases are the same. The timing shifts, but the sequence doesn't. Research and self-assessment happens 4-6 months out. Networking starts 4 months before apps. Application prep is 2 months before deadlines. Then you sequence your applications strategically: second-tier firms first for practice, dream firms last.

Interview and Offer Phases

Interviews

  • Timing: 2-6 weeks after applications close
  • First round: Two case interviews plus a fit conversation
  • Final round: Three to four interviews over a half day
  • Pace: Moves fast once the process begins

Offers

  • Timing: 1-2 weeks after your final round
  • Decision window: Typically 2-4 weeks to decide
  • Competing offers: Firms may extend the window, but don't count on it
  • Key move: Negotiate from a position of multiple offers

Three Timing Mistakes to Avoid

  • Late networking: First outreach two weeks before the deadline is not networking, it is asking a stranger for a favor. Start early.
  • Wrong sequencing: Your first application should not be to your dream firm. Apply to practice firms first, then bring your A game.
  • Missing early pipelines: Diversity and early ID programs have deadlines months before the standard cycle. If eligible, these are not optional.
4:30 - 5:30 | Three timing mistakes that sink more candidates than bad case math. First, late networking. If your first outreach is two weeks before the deadline, that's asking a stranger for a favor, not networking. Second, wrong sequencing. Your first application should not be to your top choice. Apply to practice firms first. Third, missing early pipelines. Diversity recruiting and early ID programs have deadlines months earlier, and by the time you hear about them through normal channels, they've often passed.

Key Takeaways

What to Remember

Know your specific timeline: undergrad, MBA, or experienced hire. Pull up your target firms' career pages and write down every deadline.

Start networking 4-6 months before applications open. The candidates who get referrals built relationships when they didn't need anything.

Sequence strategically: practice firms first, dream firms last. Early pipelines before standard deadlines. If you're an experienced hire, create your own urgency.

5:50 - 6:00 | Three things to lock in. Know your specific timeline and check each firm individually. Start networking before you think you're ready. And sequence strategically: practice firms first, dream firms last, early pipelines before standard deadlines, and if you're an experienced hire, set your own deadline because nobody else will create that urgency for you.
