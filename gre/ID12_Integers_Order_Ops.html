Signed Number Rules

Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division

Signed Numbers

Addition Rules

Same Signs

Add the absolute values, keep the sign.

$6 + 3 = $ $9$

$(-6) + (-3) = $ $-9$

Different Signs

Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger. Keep the larger's sign.

$6 + (-3) = $ $3$ (positive wins)

$(-6) + 3 = $ $-3$ (negative wins)

Signed Numbers

Subtraction: Add the Opposite

Subtracting a negative is adding a positive. The two negatives cancel.

Expression Rewrite as Addition Result
$6 - 3$ $6 + (-3)$ $3$
$6 - (-3)$ $6 + (+3)$ $9$
$-6 - 3$ $-6 + (-3)$ $-9$

Special Cases the GRE Loves

Multiply by Zero

$a \times 0 = 0$

Anything times zero is always zero.

Zero Divided

$\frac{0}{a} = 0$

Zero divided by any nonzero number is zero.

Divide by Zero

$\frac{a}{0} = \text{?}$

UNDEFINED (not zero!)

3:40-4:15 | Three special cases the GRE loves to test. Anything times zero is zero. Zero divided by any number is zero. But dividing by zero is undefined, not zero. This is a classic trap.

Order of Operations

Two Classic GRE Traps

Trap 1: Left-to-Right

$24 \div 4 \times 2$

Wrong: $24 \div 8 = 3$

Right: $6 \times 2 = $ $12$

Trap 2: Negative-Squared

Watch where the negative sign sits:

$(-3)^2 = (-3)(-3) = $ $9$

$-3^2 = -(3^2) = $ $-9$

Parentheses matter!

5:15-5:55 | Two classic traps. Trap 1: Division and multiplication go left to right, so 24 divided by 4 is 6, then times 2 is 12. Trap 2: Negative squared depends on parentheses. With parentheses around the negative 3, the answer is 9. Without parentheses, it is negative 9.

Order of Operations

Nested Grouping Symbols

Work from the innermost grouping outward.

Problem

$-[12 - (-4 - 3)]^2$

Solution

Step 1: Innermost: $(-4 - 3) = -7$

Step 2: Brackets: $[12 - (-7)] = 19$

Step 3: Negative and exponent: $-(19)^2$

Answer: $-361$

Fraction bars also count as grouping symbols. Evaluate the entire numerator and denominator before dividing.

Example 3

The Left-to-Right Trap

Problem

$25 \div 5 \cdot 5 + 8 - 5$

Solution

M/D left to right: $25 \div 5 = 5$, then $5 \cdot 5 = 25$

A/S left to right: $25 + 8 = 33$, then $33 - 5$

Answer: $28$

Example 4

Nested Grouping Symbols

Problem

$4[6 + (6 \div 2)^2] - 1$

Solution

Innermost first: $6 \div 2 = 3$

Exponent: $3^2 = 9$

Brackets: $6 + 9 = 15$

Multiply then subtract: $4 \cdot 15 = 60$, then $60 - 1$

Answer: $59$

Example 5

Dividing Negative Fractions

Problem

$\left(\frac{-6}{7}\right) \div \left(\frac{-41}{42}\right)$

Solution

Multiply by reciprocal: $\frac{-6}{7} \times \frac{42}{-41}$

Same signs (both negative) $\rightarrow$ positive: $\frac{6 \times 42}{7 \times 41}$

Simplify: $\frac{252}{287} = \frac{36}{41}$

Answer: $\frac{36}{41}$

Example 6

GRE-Style Expression

Problem

$[(3 - 6)^2 + 4]^2 + 3 \cdot 4 - 12 \div 3$

Solution

Step 1: Innermost: $(3 - 6) = -3$

Step 2: Exponent & brackets: $(-3)^2 = 9$, then $[9 + 4] = 13$

Step 3: Outer exponent: $13^2 = 169$; M/D: $3 \cdot 4 = 12$, $12 \div 3 = 4$

Answer: $169 + 12 - 4 =$ $177$

Summary

Integers & Order of Operations

  • Same-sign addition: Add absolute values and keep the common sign
  • Different-sign addition: Subtract absolutes and keep the larger's sign
  • Subtraction: Convert to adding the opposite, then apply addition rules
  • Multiply & divide signs: Same signs give positive, different signs give negative
  • PEMDAS left to right: M/D share equal priority (left to right), same for A/S
  • Negative-squared trap: $(-3)^2 = 9$ but $-3^2 = -9$; parentheses matter
