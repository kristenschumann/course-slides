Pre-MBA Internships

Two Types of Opportunities

Structured Programs

Formal programs by large firms, primarily consulting and banking. Short (days to weeks), happen in the summer before school. Some lead directly to summer internship offers.

Self-Sourced

Opportunities you find through networking. More common in tech and startups. Valuable for building experience and connections, but typically no pipeline to future offers.

Pre-MBA internships are not required for MBA success. Do them if they align with your goals, not because of peer pressure.

Part 1

Networking Strategy

How to build your network before school starts

5:30 - 5:35 | Whether or not you do a formal program, the networking you do before school starts is critical. Let's talk about how to approach it.

Networking Actions

Build Relationships Systematically

  • Join class channels: Slack, WhatsApp, Discord, LinkedIn. Get on all platforms to find where your cohort is active.
  • Reach out to second-years: They just went through recruiting. Ask what they wish they'd known.
  • Connect with alumni: Request 20-minute informational interviews at target companies. Follow up with thanks.
  • Update LinkedIn: Add your school and class year. Connect with classmates and engage with school content.
5:35 - 7:15 | Four networking actions. Join all class channels: Slack, WhatsApp, Discord, LinkedIn groups. You'll figure out which one your cohort uses. Reach out to second-year students in your target industry. They just went through the recruiting process and are a goldmine of advice. Connect with recent alumni at target companies for short informational interviews. Keep them to 20 minutes, come prepared, and follow up with a thank-you note. Update your LinkedIn profile to reflect where you're going. Alumni are very responsive to incoming students.

Industry Preparation

Start Preparing Over the Summer

Consulting

Start case interview basics. Build a foundation so formal prep in fall feels familiar. Network with consultants at target firms.

Banking

Start networking with bankers early. Review accounting, valuation, and modeling basics. Recruiting begins very early in fall.

Tech / PM

Practice product sense and estimation questions. Research companies and roles. Recruiting is less structured but early networking still matters.

7:15 - 8:45 | Different industries need different preparation. For consulting, start case interview basics over the summer. You don't need to be an expert by day one, but a foundation helps. For banking, start networking with bankers as early as possible. Review accounting, valuation, and modeling basics. Banking recruiting at many schools begins very early in fall, so summer is your window. For tech and product management, practice product sense and estimation questions. Recruiting is less structured and happens later, giving you more time, but early networking still pays off. Across all industries, building basic AI fluency is increasingly expected.

The Key Insight

Show Up with a Plan

The strongest recruiters aren't necessarily the ones who did pre-MBA internships. They're the ones who showed up on day one with a plan, a network, and clarity about what they wanted to pursue.

The groundwork you lay before school starts, the relationships, the preparation, the clarity about your goals, will compound throughout your MBA experience.
