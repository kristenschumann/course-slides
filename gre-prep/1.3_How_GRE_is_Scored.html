Understanding Percentiles

Verbal vs. Quant percentiles

The same score means different things on each section. Quant scores skew higher, so it takes a higher Quant score to stand out.

Score Verbal % Quant %
170 99% 94%

160 84% 61%
155 65% 46%

The Key Difference

A 160 in Verbal = 84th percentile. A 160 in Quant = only 61st percentile.

Average Scores

How It Works

Section-adaptive scoring

The GRE uses equating to adjust for question difficulty, so scores are comparable across test dates. Here is how section-level adaptation works.

Do Well on Section 1

You get a harder Section 2. Questions in the harder section are worth more toward your final score.

This is where high scores come from.

Struggle on Section 1

You get an easier Section 2. Even getting most right won't yield as high a score.

The scoring ceiling is lower.

Strategy

Don't rush Section 1. If running low on time, guess on remaining questions rather than leaving them blank.

3:30 - 5:00 | Explain section-adaptive scoring. First section performance determines second section difficulty. Doing well unlocks harder questions worth more points. Key strategy: don't rush Section 1, always guess rather than skip.

Program Expectations

What scores mean for admissions

There is no universal "good" GRE score. What matters is how yours compares to your target programs.

STEM Programs

Prioritize Quant. A 165Q with 155V might be perfectly fine.

Humanities & Social Sci

Prioritize Verbal. A 165V with 155Q might work well.

MBA & Business

Want strong scores in both. Top programs look for 160+ in each.

Action Step

Research your target programs and find their median GRE scores. Aim to match or exceed them.

5:00 - 6:30 | Different programs care about different sections. STEM focuses on Quant. Humanities on Verbal. MBA wants both. Research your specific programs to set realistic score targets.
