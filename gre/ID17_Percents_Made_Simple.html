Foundation

What Is a Percent?

Percent means "per hundred" or "out of 100." For example: $25\% = \frac{25}{100} = 0.25$

Percent Decimal Fraction
50% 0.50 $\frac{1}{2}$
25% 0.25 $\frac{1}{4}$
20% 0.20 $\frac{1}{5}$
10% 0.10 $\frac{1}{10}$
1% 0.01 $\frac{1}{100}$
0:45 - 1:45 | Introduce the core definition: percent means "per hundred." Walk through the conversion table. Students should memorize these common conversions.

Conversions

Converting Between Forms

Percent to Decimal

Divide by 100 (move decimal 2 places left)

$45\% = 0.45$

Decimal to Percent

Multiply by 100 (move decimal 2 places right)

$0.08 = 8\%$

Watch Out

$0.5\%$ is NOT $0.5$. It equals $\frac{0.5}{100} = 0.005$. Always divide by 100 first.

1:45 - 2:15 | Explain the two conversion methods. Then highlight the most common trap: 0.5% equals 0.005, not 0.5. Students must always divide by 100 when converting from percent to decimal.

Problem Types

Every Percent Problem Is One of Three

01

Find the Part

"What is P% of W?"

Part $= \frac{P}{100} \times$ Whole

02

Find the Percent

"X is what % of Y?"

% $= \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100$

03

Find the Whole

"X is P% of what?"

Whole $= \text{Part} \div \frac{P}{100}$

Key Idea

Part = Percent $\times$ Whole. Rearrange this one formula for any unknown.

4:45 - 5:45 | Categorize every basic percent problem into one of three types. Walk through each card. Emphasize the memory device: Part = Percent times Whole. Rearrange to find whichever piece is missing.

Beyond 100%

Percents Greater Than 100%

Percents can exceed 100%. This means the part is larger than the base.

  • 150% of 40: $1.50 \times 40 =$ $60$
  • 75 is what % of 50: $\frac{75}{50} \times 100 =$ $150\%$
  • 200% of what is 36: $2.00 \times x = 36$, so $x =$ $18$
Key Idea

100% = the number itself. 200% = twice the number. 50% = half.

5:45 - 6:45 | Walk through three examples with percents over 100%. No fragments here. Emphasize: 100% equals the number itself, 200% is double, 50% is half. This mental model helps sanity-check answers.

Worked Example 1

Price Markdown Problem

Problem

A shirt priced at $\$80$ is marked down by 25%. What is the sale price?

Solution

Find discount: $0.25 \times 80 = 20$

Subtract: $80 - 20 = 60$

Or: 75% of original = $0.75 \times 80$

Sale price: $\$60$

Summary

Percents Made Simple

The Translation Code

is = equals, of = multiply, what = variable, percent = divide by 100

Watch Out

$0.5\% = 0.005$, NOT $0.5$. Always convert percent to decimal before multiplying.

Three Problem Types

1. Find part: Part $= \frac{\%}{100} \times$ Whole

2. Find percent: $\% = \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100$

3. Find whole: Whole $= \text{Part} \div \frac{\%}{100}$
