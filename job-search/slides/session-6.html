Four Assessment Types

Take-Home

Take-Home Projects

Build something on your own time. 2-8 hours. Tests depth of thinking and execution quality.

Case

Case Interviews

Solve a business problem live. 30-45 min. Tests structured thinking under pressure.

Live

Live Challenges

Complete a task in real-time with the team watching. Tests how you work, not just the output.

Presentation

Presentations

Present a solution or portfolio piece. 15-30 min. Tests communication and strategic framing.

Job Search Bootcamp

AI in Assessments

Where AI Helps
  • Research and context-gathering before the assessment
  • Structuring your initial outline or approach
  • Proofreading and polishing final deliverables
Where AI Hurts
  • Generating the core analysis (evaluators can tell)
  • Copy-pasting AI output without your own perspective
  • Using AI during live sessions where originality is expected
12:00 - 14:00 | Walk through both columns. Be direct about the risks. COACH: "AI is a tool, not a shortcut. Use it to prepare and polish. Never use it to replace your thinking. Evaluators have seen enough AI-generated case responses to spot them instantly." Key point: AI for preparation = smart. AI for execution = risky.

Job Search Bootcamp

The Golden Rule

Use AI to prepare better, not to do less work.

The best candidates use AI to research the company, understand the market, and structure their thinking before the assessment starts. Then they bring their own perspective to the work itself.

14:00 - 15:00 | Key insight. Let it land. COACH: "This is the line. AI for prep, yes. AI for the deliverable itself, no. If your take-home reads like ChatGPT wrote it, that's worse than a B-minus original. Hiring managers want to see how you think."

Job Search Bootcamp

Step 1: Clarify

01

Read Twice

First for comprehension, second for constraints. Highlight deadlines, word limits, and deliverable format.

02

Ask Questions

If clarification is allowed, ask. It shows thoroughness, not weakness.

03

Find the Real Question

The prompt says "analyze market trends." The real question is "can you think strategically about our business?"

21:30 - 23:30 | Deep dive on Step 1. Use the "real question" example to make it concrete. COACH: "Most people rush past this step. They read the prompt once, start working, and realize halfway through they missed something. Clarify first. It takes 10 minutes and saves you hours."

Job Search Bootcamp

Step 2: Structure

  • Write a one-sentence thesis before you start
  • Break the problem into 3-4 clear sections
  • Allocate time to each section (be honest about what you can cover)
  • Decide what you will NOT include (scope is a skill)
  • For live assessments, say your structure out loud before diving in
23:30 - 25:30 | Walk through the structure step. Emphasize scoping. COACH: "Structure is where the magic happens. An imperfect answer with great structure beats a brilliant answer with no structure. Why? Because structure shows you can think. And that's what they're hiring for." Key point: Saying what you won't cover is as powerful as what you will.

Job Search Bootcamp

Steps 3-4: Execute and Polish

Execute

  • Follow your structure. Don't deviate.
  • Flag assumptions rather than getting stuck
  • Leave 15-20% of your time for polishing

Polish + Present

  • Proofread for clarity, not perfection
  • Add an executive summary or key takeaways
  • Prepare to explain your reasoning, not just your answer
25:30 - 28:00 | Walk through Execute and Polish together. Keep it practical. COACH: "Execute is about discipline. Follow the plan. Don't go down rabbit holes. And always, always leave time to polish. A polished B-plus beats a rough A-minus every time." Key point: Leave time at the end. Always.

Job Search Bootcamp

The Secret Step

Step 5: Present your thinking, not just your answer.

Every assessment is also a communication test. Walk evaluators through your reasoning. Show how you structured the problem, what trade-offs you made, and why. This is what separates candidates who "did the work" from candidates who "think like us."

28:00 - 30:00 | Key insight on presentation. Pause for emphasis. COACH: "This is the step most people skip. They submit the deliverable and stop. The best candidates present their process, not just their output. 'Here's how I approached this, here's what I prioritized, and here's why.' That's what gets you hired." TRANSITION: "Now let's put this framework to work. You're about to do a timed practice assessment."
