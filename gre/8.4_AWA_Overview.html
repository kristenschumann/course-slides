The AWA

One essay, 30 minutes

The Analytical Writing Assessment is the first section of the GRE. You write a single essay in response to a prompt you will not see until test day.

The Task

Analyze an Issue: take a position on a claim and support it with reasoning and examples.

Time Limit

30 minutes. No breaks before or after. It always comes first on test day.

Score Range

Scored 0 to 6 in half-point increments. Reported separately from Verbal and Quant.

What changed: Before September 2023, the GRE had two essays (Issue + Argument). ETS removed the Argument Essay, so you now write only the Issue Essay.

Scoring Rubric

Four criteria scorers evaluate

Quality of Reasoning

How well you analyze the issue, consider complexities, and develop your position.

Use of Evidence

Whether your examples and reasons are specific, relevant, and clearly connected to your thesis.

Organization

Clear structure with logical flow. Each paragraph serves a purpose and transitions are smooth.

Language Facility

Sentence variety, appropriate vocabulary, and grammar. Minor errors are fine if they do not obscure meaning.

Key insight: Reasoning and evidence matter most. You do not need perfect grammar or creative prose to score well.

3:00 - 4:30 | Break down the four criteria. Emphasize that reasoning and evidence carry the most weight. Students who focus on clear thinking and specific examples will outscore students who focus on fancy vocabulary. For detailed Issue Essay writing strategy, see Lesson 8.5.

Scoring Process

How your essay is actually graded

Your essay is scored by one human reader and one automated system called e-rater.

Human Reader

A trained ETS scorer reads your essay holistically and assigns a score from 0 to 6.

E-rater (Automated)

An AI system evaluates grammar, usage, mechanics, style, organization, and development.

Disagreement rule: If the human and e-rater scores differ by more than 1 point, a second human reader scores the essay. Your final score is the average of the two closest scores.

4:30 - 6:00 | Explain the dual scoring system. The e-rater is good at catching mechanical issues (grammar, spelling, sentence structure) but the human reader evaluates reasoning quality. This is why clear organization helps you with both scorers. Clean structure helps the e-rater, and clear reasoning helps the human reader.

Scoring Process

What e-rater evaluates

Grammar

Subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, pronoun reference.

Usage

Correct word choice and idiomatic expressions.

Mechanics

Spelling, capitalization, and punctuation.

Style

Sentence variety, appropriate register, and word diversity.

Organization

Paragraph structure, transitions, and logical flow.

Development

Depth of ideas, supporting detail, and essay length.

Practical tip: Use clear paragraph breaks and vary your sentence length. These are easy wins for the e-rater that cost you no extra time.

6:00 - 7:00 | Walk through the six areas e-rater checks. The actionable takeaway: write in clear paragraphs, vary your sentence structure, and proofread for obvious spelling and grammar errors. You do not need to be a great writer, but you do need to be clean.

Key Takeaways

Analytical writing overview

One essay, 30 minutes. The GRE AWA is now a single Issue Essay (Argument Essay removed September 2023).

Scored 0-6 based on reasoning, evidence, organization, and language. Reasoning and evidence matter most.

Human + e-rater. One trained reader and one automated system score your essay. Clean structure helps with both.

4.0+ is the target for most programs. That puts you above the 50th percentile with room to spare.

Do not over-prepare. Verbal and Quant scores carry far more weight. Practice 2-3 essays and move on.
