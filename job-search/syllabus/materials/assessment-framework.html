Session 6 Resource

Assessment Framework Reference

A one-page reference card for the 5-step framework that works for case studies, take-home projects, and presentations.

The 5-Step Framework

1

Clarify

5-10% of time

Read the brief twice. List your assumptions. Identify exactly what "done" looks like. Ask clarifying questions if you can.

Common mistake: Jumping in before understanding the real question.

2

Structure

10-15% of time

Create an outline before building anything. Break the problem into 3-4 components. Map your approach to what they're evaluating.

Common mistake: Going deep on one area before seeing the whole picture.

3

Execute

50-60% of time

Work your structure from top to bottom. Prioritize the 2-3 areas with the most impact. Show your work -- partial credit matters.

Common mistake: Spending too long on polish before the substance is complete.

4

Polish

10-15% of time

Check for errors and consistency. Ensure your narrative is clear. Add a brief executive summary if relevant.

Common mistake: Skipping this step because you ran out of time.

5

Present

10-15% of time

Lead with your recommendation, then show your work. Anticipate the top 3 questions they'll ask. Stay calm on follow-ups -- they're testing how you think, not if you're perfect.

Common mistake: Presenting chronologically instead of leading with the answer.

By Assessment Type

Assessment Type What They Say What They're Really Testing Key Tip
Case Study "Analyze this business problem" Structured thinking, prioritization, business judgment Lead with a hypothesis, then test it
Take-Home Project "Complete this by Friday" Quality of work, communication, time management Spend 60% on substance, 20% on presentation, 20% on polish
Live Challenge "Solve this in 45 minutes" How you think under pressure, collaboration style Narrate your thinking out loud
Presentation "Present your findings" Communication skills, executive presence, storytelling Practice out loud 3x minimum

Quick-Reference Checklist

Before You Start

  • Read brief twice
  • List assumptions
  • Define "done"
  • Set time allocations

While Working

  • Follow your structure
  • Prioritize high-impact areas
  • Check the clock at 50% time
  • Leave buffer for polish

Before Submitting

  • Proofread everything
  • Executive summary present
  • Narrative is clear
  • Would you hire someone who submitted this?
