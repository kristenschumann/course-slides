0:00-0:20 | Hook: Three ways to describe the "center" of data: mean, median, mode. Each tells a different story. The GRE loves testing when to use which, and how to find missing values.

Central Tendency

Three Ways to Find the Center

Mean

$\bar{x} = \frac{\Sigma x}{n}$

Sum of values divided by the count.

Median

Middle value

Arrange in order, find the center position.

Mode

Most frequent

The value that appears most often.

0:20-1:00 | Introduce the three measures. Mean is the arithmetic average. Median is the middle value when sorted. Mode is the most frequent value. Each captures a different aspect of center.

Example 1

Calculating the Mean

Problem

Find the mean of 4, 7, 10, 15, 14.

Solution

Sum: $4 + 7 + 10 + 15 + 14 = 50$

Count: $n = 5$

Mean $= \frac{50}{5} =$ $10$

1:00-1:30 | Example 1: Calculate the mean. Click to reveal solution. Sum all values to get 50. Divide by 5 values. Mean equals 10.

Key Insight

Sum = Mean x Count

$\text{Sum} = \bar{x} \times n$

This rearrangement is powerful for finding missing values on the GRE.

Problem

The average of 5 numbers is 12. Four of them are 8, 10, 15, and 13. What is the fifth?

Solution

Total sum: $12 \times 5 = 60$

Known sum: $8 + 10 + 15 + 13 = 46$

Fifth number $= 60 - 46 =$ $14$

1:30-2:30 | The key formula: Sum equals Mean times Count. This lets you find missing values. Example: average of 5 numbers is 12, so total is 60. Known sum is 46. Missing number is 14.

Median

Finding the Middle Value

Odd Count

Data: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9

Median is the middle number.

Median $=$ $5$

Even Count

Data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12

Average the two middle numbers.

Median $= \frac{6+8}{2} =$ $7$

Watch Out

Always arrange data in order first. An unsorted list gives the wrong median.

2:30-3:30 | Median: the middle value when sorted. For odd count, take the center number. For even count, average the two center numbers. Always sort first.

Choosing the Right Measure

Mean vs. Median vs. Mode

Measure Best When Weakness
Mean Data is symmetric, no outliers Pulled by extreme values
Median Outliers present (income, prices) Ignores actual values
Mode Categorical data (most popular item) May not exist or be unique
GRE Favorite

Outliers pull the mean toward them but leave the median unchanged. The GRE frequently tests this distinction.

3:30-4:15 | When to use which measure. Mean for symmetric data. Median when outliers are present. Mode for categorical data. The GRE loves testing the effect of outliers on mean vs median.

Part 2

Weighted Averages

When not all values count equally

4:15-4:25 | Transition: Sometimes different values carry different weight. That is where weighted averages come in.

Example 3

Weighted Average: Course Grade

Problem

A student scores 80 on the midterm (40% of grade) and 90 on the final (60% of grade). What is the course average?

Solution

$\text{WA} = \frac{\Sigma(\text{value} \times \text{weight})}{\Sigma(\text{weights})}$

$= \frac{80(0.40) + 90(0.60)}{0.40 + 0.60}$

$= \frac{32 + 54}{1}$

Answer: $86$

4:25-5:15 | Weighted average formula. Multiply each value by its weight, sum the products, divide by the sum of the weights. The course average is 86, not the simple average of 85.

Example 4

Combining Two Groups

Problem

Class A: 20 students, average 75. Class B: 30 students, average 85. What is the overall average?

Solution

Class A total: $20 \times 75 = 1{,}500$

Class B total: $30 \times 85 = 2{,}550$

Overall: $\frac{4{,}050}{50}$

Answer: $81$ (not 80!)

5:15-6:15 | Combining groups: you cannot simply average the averages. Class B has more students, so the overall average is pulled toward 85. The answer is 81, not 80.

Summary

Mean, Median, Mode & Weighted Averages

  • Mean -- Sum divided by count; sensitive to outliers
  • Median -- Middle value when sorted; resistant to outliers
  • Mode -- Most frequent value; useful for categorical data
  • Key formula -- Sum = Mean x Count for finding missing values
  • Weighted average -- Multiply each value by its weight, then divide by total weight
  • Never average averages -- When combining groups, weight by group size
6:15-7:00 | Summary: Mean, median, and mode each capture a different center. Use Sum = Mean x Count for missing values. For weighted averages, always weight by group size.
