Essentials

What to bring and what to leave

Bring

  • Valid photo ID (must match registration name, not expired)
  • Confirmation email or appointment number
  • Snacks and water for the break
  • Layers of clothing (centers can be cold)
  • Earplugs if sensitive to noise

Do Not Bring

  • Watches of any kind (even analog)
  • Phone in testing room (must be stored)
  • Outside scratch paper or notes
  • Food or drink in testing room
1:30 - 3:00 | Go through the bring/don't-bring list. Emphasize the ID requirement: it must match your registration name exactly, have a photo and signature, and not be expired. This is the most common test-day problem.

Morning Routine

Test day morning

Physical Prep

  • Wake up with plenty of time
  • Balanced breakfast (protein + carbs)
  • Normal caffeine only
  • Use the bathroom before leaving

Mental Prep

  • Review your top 3 reminders briefly
  • Do not learn anything new
  • Some nervousness is normal
  • Trust your preparation
Key Idea

Arrive 30 minutes early. Present ID, store personal items, and settle in without rushing.

During the Test

Section strategy reminders

  • First section of each type matters most. Take your time on the first Verbal and first Quant sections.
  • Mark and move if stuck. Do not spend 5 minutes on one question. Flag it and come back.
  • Use process of elimination. Even removing two choices improves your odds.
  • Answer every question. There is no penalty for guessing on the GRE.
  • Save 2-3 minutes at the end to review any marked questions in each section.
5:30 - 7:00 | Five key reminders for during the test. The first section is adaptive and sets difficulty for the second section, so it deserves extra care. Never leave a question blank. GRE tip: if you are running low on time, spend your last 30 seconds clicking an answer for every unanswered question. Random guessing gives you a 20-25% chance per question, and there is zero penalty for wrong answers.

Break and After

Reset and finish strong

During the Break

  • Use the bathroom
  • Eat your snack, drink water
  • Stretch and move around
  • Do not review or analyze

After the Test

  • See unofficial V and Q scores
  • Decide: accept or cancel
  • Choose schools (up to 4 free)
  • Official scores in 8-10 days
Tip

Unless something went terribly wrong, accept your scores. Retake and use ScoreSelect later.

7:00 - 8:30 | Cover break strategy (reset, don't analyze) and post-test steps (accept scores, send to schools). Mention ScoreSelect as a safety net. Pro tip: during the break, do NOT discuss the test with anyone or try to look up answers on your phone. Dwelling on a hard question you already answered wastes mental energy and can hurt performance on remaining sections.

Contingencies

If something goes wrong

Running Late

Call the test center. They may accommodate slight delays.

Feeling Unwell

Cancel and reschedule. Better to test at full capacity.

Tech Issues

Raise your hand. Time is usually paused for technical problems.

Remember

Stay calm. These situations are handled regularly at test centers. You have options.

8:30 - 9:30 | Cover three emergency scenarios. The key message: you always have options. No single test-day problem is catastrophic.

At-Home Testing

Requirements for testing at home

  • Computer with webcam, mic, and speakers. Windows or Mac. No headphones allowed.
  • Private, enclosed room. Door closed, no other people, clear desk with no papers or secondary monitors.
  • Whiteboard replaces scratch paper. Physical whiteboard allowed. Must show both sides to proctor before and after.
  • Reliable internet connection. Test your connection speed the day before to avoid surprises.
9:30 - 10:30 | Cover at-home testing requirements. Many students now take the GRE at home. The key differences: no scratch paper (whiteboard only), no headphones, and the room must be completely private with a closed door. Emphasize testing equipment the day before. If the webcam or mic fails during the test, the session may be terminated.

At-Home Testing

What to expect on test day

Check In Early

Log in via ETS browser 15-20 minutes before your appointment time.

Room and ID Scan

Show 360-degree room scan with webcam and hold valid ID to camera.

Proctor Monitors You

Live proctor watches via webcam throughout. Same 10-minute break as test center.

Scores Are Identical

No difference to schools. At-home and test-center scores are treated the same.

Prep Tip

Test your equipment the day before. Have a backup internet plan. Practice working with a whiteboard instead of scratch paper.

10:30 - 11:30 | Walk through the at-home testing process. The check-in takes 15-20 minutes, so log in early. The room scan is thorough: they will ask you to show under your desk, behind your monitor, etc. If your connection drops, you may be able to resume at the proctor's discretion, but this is not guaranteed. Key reassurance: scores are identical whether you test at home or at a center. Schools cannot tell the difference.

Key Takeaways

Test-day checklist

  • Night before: confirm logistics, lay out everything, get 7-8 hours of sleep, do not cram
  • Bring: valid photo ID, confirmation number, snacks and water, layers of clothing
  • Morning: eat a balanced breakfast, review your top 3 reminders only, arrive 30 minutes early
  • During the test: first section matters most, mark and move when stuck, answer every question
  • Break: eat, stretch, breathe, reset mentally. Do not analyze how the test is going.
  • At home: webcam, private room, whiteboard instead of scratch paper. Test equipment the day before.
  • After: accept your scores unless something went seriously wrong. You completed an 8-week journey.
