The Reality

How programs use writing scores

Key Insight

The AWA is more "check the box" than "higher is always better." A 4.5 vs 5.5 won't matter. But a 3.0 when peers have 4.5+ raises questions.

Humanities

Writing matters more. You'll be doing lots of academic writing in these programs.

MBA Programs

Some care about writing, especially top programs. Check your specific targets.

STEM Programs

Generally care less, but a very low score still looks bad on your application.

0:30 - 2:00 | Most programs weight AWA less than Verbal/Quant. But a very low score can hurt. Humanities cares most, STEM cares least, but a 3.0 looks bad anywhere.

Target Scores

What score do you need?

Score What It Means
5.0+ Strong score. Unlikely to be a concern for any program.
4.5 Comfortable margin. Above about 80% of test-takers.
4.0 Safe for most programs. Around the 60th percentile.
<4.0 Potential red flag, especially if rest of application doesn't show strong writing.
Good News

If you're a native English speaker with decent writing skills, you can probably hit 4.0+ with minimal prep.

2:00 - 3:00 | Score benchmarks: 5.0+ is strong, 4.5 is comfortable, 4.0 is safe for most programs. Below 4.0 is a red flag. Native English speakers can usually hit 4.0 with minimal prep.

The Essentials

Minimum prep for everyone

Even if writing isn't your priority, do at least this much. It's maybe 2-3 hours total.

1. Know the Task

Analyze an Issue (give your opinion on a topic). 30 minutes.

2. Know the Scoring

ETS wants clear organization, specific examples, and logical development. Not creative writing or perfect grammar.

3. Practice Once

Write at least one essay under timed conditions. Don't see the format for the first time on test day.

Structure Tip

Intro-body-body-body-conclusion works fine. Clear beats creative.

3:00 - 4:30 | Three minimum prep steps: know the task (Issue Essay only, since the Argument Essay was removed in September 2023), know the scoring criteria, and practice at least once under timed conditions. Have a basic structure ready. That's 2-3 hours total.

Extra Preparation

When to do more

Do more prep if:

  • Your diagnostic essay score is 3.5 or below
  • Writing in English is challenging for you
  • Your target programs emphasize writing ability
  • Your other materials also need work

If any apply, budget time to:

  • Study sample essays at different score levels
  • Practice 3-4 Issue essays
  • Get feedback from someone else
  • Focus on clear structure and specific examples
4:30 - 5:30 | Do more if: diagnostic below 3.5, English is challenging, programs emphasize writing, or other materials need work. Study sample essays, practice 3-4 Issue essays, get feedback, focus on structure.

Summary

Do you need to study writing?

  • Most programs care less about AWA than Verbal and Quant, but a very low score can still hurt you
  • Aim for 4.0 or above - this is safe for most programs and around the 60th percentile
  • Everyone should do minimum prep - know the task (Issue Essay), know the criteria, practice at least once
  • Do more if writing is a challenge - practice multiple essays and get feedback from someone else
  • For most students, a few hours is enough - put your main energy into Verbal and Quant
5:30 - 6:00 | Summary: Most programs weight AWA less. Below 4.0 is a red flag. Everyone should do minimum prep. Do more if writing is challenging or important to your programs. For most students, a few hours is enough. [Leland+ Tip] Write two practice Issue essays using official ETS prompts and time yourself strictly at 30 minutes each. Most students who score below 4.0 simply never practiced under timed conditions. Structure matters more than brilliance: a clear thesis, two concrete examples, and a brief conclusion will reliably score 4.0 or above. Save your deep study hours for Verbal and Quant, where score improvements have far more admissions impact.
