The Stakes

Most students lose verbal points not because they lack vocabulary or reading skill, but because they fall into predictable traps under time pressure.

Students repeat the same 3-4 mistakes across every Verbal section, costing 5-10 points they could easily recover.

Good News

Reading Passages

Mistakes 1-2: how you read the passage

Mistake 1

Reading too carefully

Spending 4-5 minutes on a passage trying to understand every detail before looking at questions.

Fix

Read for structure in 2-3 minutes. Know WHERE information is, not WHAT every sentence says.

Mistake 2

Not mapping the passage

Diving into questions without noting paragraph purposes, transitions, or the author's stance.

Fix

Mentally tag each paragraph: main claim, evidence, counterpoint, conclusion. This takes 10 seconds.

1:30 - 4:00 | Two passage-level mistakes. Mistake 1: reading too carefully wastes time and doesn't help, because you'll re-read for each question anyway. The goal is a structural map, not memorization. Mistake 2: without a mental map, you waste time hunting for evidence. - Slow readers often re-read the entire passage for every question, compounding the time loss - A good mental map means noting: "Paragraph 1 = main claim, Paragraph 2 = evidence, Paragraph 3 = counterargument" - Practice this on every passage during prep until it becomes automatic

Answer Selection

Mistakes 3-4: how you pick answers

Mistake 3

Picking "sounds right" answers

Choosing answers that feel correct intuitively instead of checking passage evidence.

Fix

For every RC answer, ask: "Can I point to a specific line that supports this?" If not, it is probably wrong.

Mistake 4

Not using process of elimination

Scanning for the "right" answer instead of systematically removing wrong ones.

Fix

Eliminate first, then choose. Removing 3 wrong answers is easier than spotting 1 right one.

4:00 - 6:30 | Two answer-selection mistakes. Mistake 3: the GRE designs wrong answers to "sound right." The only defense is evidence-based selection. Mistake 4: elimination is more reliable than recognition, especially under pressure. - "Sounds right" is the number one trap on RC, especially with outside knowledge - Process of elimination works because wrong answers have identifiable flaws: too extreme, reverses the argument, or adds unsupported claims - Make elimination a habit during practice so it is automatic on test day

Text Completion and SE

Mistakes 5-6: signal words and vocabulary

Mistake 5

Ignoring signal words

Missing transition words like "however," "despite," "moreover" that determine the blank's direction.

Fix

Circle every signal word before looking at choices. The signal tells you whether the blank continues or reverses the clue.

Mistake 6

Skipping vocabulary study

Hoping context clues will be enough without building a working vocabulary of high-frequency GRE words.

Fix

Learn 300-500 high-frequency words. Focus on words that appear repeatedly in GRE practice materials.

6:30 - 9:00 | Two TC/SE mistakes. Mistake 5: signal words are the single most important clue in TC and SE. "However" means the blank reverses the previous idea. "Moreover" means it continues. Students who skip signals guess instead of solving. Mistake 6: you can't rely on context alone. The GRE tests vocabulary directly, and 300-500 targeted words cover most of what appears. - Practice identifying signal words on every TC/SE question during prep - For vocabulary, use spaced repetition (flashcards) rather than cramming word lists - Focus on words you see repeatedly in official GRE materials, not obscure lists

Inference and Practice

Mistakes 7-8: overthinking and under-practicing

Mistake 7

Overthinking inference questions

Adding spin to inferences by bringing in outside knowledge or making logical leaps.

Fix

Apply the no-spin rule: the right answer takes the smallest logical step from the text. If you cannot prove it from the passage, it is wrong.

Mistake 8

Not practicing with real GRE passages

Reading articles or other sources to improve reading comprehension instead of just practicing GRE passages and questions.

Fix

Use ETS Official Guide and PowerPrep tests when you can. Use third-party RC passages to supplement.

9:00 - 11:30 | Two more mistakes. Mistake 7: inference questions test whether you can resist adding spin. The right answer is always the most conservative, most directly supported option. Mistake 8: third-party passages don't replicate how ETS writes wrong answers, so you build the wrong instincts. - The no-spin rule: add nothing, subtract nothing. If you can't point to text, it's wrong - ETS has a specific style of wrong answers (half-right, too extreme, true but not stated) that third-party materials don't replicate well - At minimum, do all official RC questions before test day

Time and Strategy

Mistakes 9-10: pacing and prioritization

Mistake 9

Poor time management

Spending too long on hard questions early and rushing through easier ones at the end.

Fix

Budget ~1.5 minutes per TC/SE and ~2 minutes per RC question. Mark and move after 2.5 minutes.

Mistake 10

Treating all questions as equal

Giving a difficult 3-blank TC the same time as a straightforward SE or short RC question.

Fix

Triage: do easy questions first, flag hard ones, and return with remaining time. Every question is worth the same point.

11:30 - 14:00 | Final two mistakes. Mistake 9: time management is the silent score killer. Students spend 4 minutes on one hard question and then rush the last 5. Mistake 10: not all questions are equally difficult, but they are all worth the same. Triage saves points. - A strict 2.5-minute cutoff prevents spiraling on any single question - Easy questions you rush through at the end are points you are giving away - Triage order: SE first (fastest), short RC second, TC third, long RC last

Action Plan

How to fix these before test day

Week 1-2

Identify your top 3 mistakes by reviewing your last practice test. Track which errors you make most.

Week 3-4

Do timed practice sets focused on your weak spots. Apply one fix per session until it becomes automatic.

Final Week

Take a full practice test under real conditions. Verify your fixes hold under pressure and adjust pacing.

Remember

You do not need to fix all ten. Fixing your top three will have the biggest impact on your score.

The final week should be a full-conditions practice test to verify. - Self-diagnosis is the most important step: review wrong answers and categorize the error - Don't try to fix everything at once; focus on the highest-frequency mistakes first - A practice test under real conditions is the best way to test your fixes

Key Takeaways

Top 10 verbal mistakes to avoid

  • Read for structure, not detail and build a mental map of each passage before answering questions
  • Demand passage evidence for every answer and use process of elimination instead of intuition
  • Never ignore signal words in TC and SE, as they determine whether the blank continues or reverses the clue
  • Build vocabulary deliberately with 300-500 high-frequency words using spaced repetition
  • Apply the no-spin rule on inference questions and practice with official ETS materials only
  • Manage your time ruthlessly by triaging questions and enforcing a 2.5-minute cutoff per question

Awareness plus targeted practice is all it takes. - Verbal improvement is about strategy and habits, not just studying more - Pick your top 3 mistakes and fix those first - Every point you recover from process errors is a point you already earned
