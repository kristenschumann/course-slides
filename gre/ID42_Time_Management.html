The Problem

Text Completion Looks Simple...

Just fill in the blank, right? But here's what trips people up:

They try to guess the missing word before reading the whole sentence. That's backwards.

Today's method: Use the sentence's structure and context clues to PREDICT what belongs in each blank BEFORE you look at the answer choices.

0:10 - 0:45 | Hook explaining the common mistake. Set up the contrast between guessing and strategic prediction.

Know the Format

Three Types of Text Completion

01

Single-Blank

One blank, 5 answer choices. Tests your ability to understand sentence meaning and find the word that fits.

02

Double-Blank

Two blanks, 3 choices each. You must get BOTH blanks right. No partial credit.

03

Triple-Blank

Three blanks, 3 choices each. All three must be correct. These take the most time.

Good news: Regardless of how many blanks, the strategy is the same. You're always looking for clues that tell you what kind of word belongs.

0:45 - 1:45 | Explain the three types. Emphasize no partial credit for multi-blank questions.

The Method

The Predict-Then-Match Method

1

Read for Structure and Meaning

Read the entire sentence BEFORE focusing on any blank. Understand the overall meaning and logical structure.

2

Find the Clue

Every blank has a clue somewhere in the sentence. It might indicate similar meaning, opposite meaning, cause/effect, or an example.

3

Predict Your Own Word

BEFORE looking at choices, predict what word should go in the blank. Simple words like "good," "bad," "increase" work fine.

4

Match Your Prediction

Now look at the choices and find the one that matches. If your prediction was "criticize" and you see "censure," that's likely your answer.

Signal Words

Same-Direction Signals

These words tell you the blank matches or continues the idea in the clue.

and also similarly likewise moreover in fact indeed thus therefore : (colon)

Example

"The research was thorough and _______, leaving no aspect of the topic unexamined."

"And" tells us the blank should match "thorough." Prediction: comprehensive, exhaustive, complete.

3:15 - 4:15 | Introduce same-direction signal words. Walk through the example showing how "and" signals matching.

Signal Words

Opposite-Direction Signals

These words tell you the blank contrasts with the clue. Your prediction should be the OPPOSITE.

but however although though yet despite while whereas rather surprisingly

Example

"Though she was known for her _______ manner, she surprised everyone with her warm greeting."

"Though" signals contrast with "warm greeting." Prediction: cold, unfriendly, aloof.

Time Management

How Long to Spend on Each Type

Question Type Target Time Strategy
Single-Blank TC 30-45 sec Read, identify clue, predict, match
Double-Blank TC 60-90 sec Start with the easier blank first
Triple-Blank TC 90-120 sec Use clear blanks to narrow the third
Sentence Equivalence 45-60 sec Look for synonym pairs first

Key principle: Vocab questions should be faster than RC. If you're spending 2+ minutes on any single question, make your best guess and move on.

Summary

Master Text Completion

1.

Read the whole sentence first

2.

Find signal words (same-direction or contrast)

3.

4.

Predict BEFORE looking at choices

5.

Match your prediction to an answer

6.

For multiple blanks, solve the easiest one first

7.

Always verify the completed sentence makes sense

Build this habit, and TC becomes systematic rather than random.
