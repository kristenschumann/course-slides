Job Search Bootcamp

Build Phase 1: Prompt + First Run

  1. Open your AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or similar)
  2. Write your prompt: describe the input, give clear instructions, provide an example
  3. Run it with real or sample data
  4. Review the output. What's good? What's missing?

Starter prompt if you're stuck: "I have [type of input, e.g. meeting notes]. Please [action, e.g. extract action items with owners and deadlines]. Format the output as [format, e.g. a numbered list]."

33:00 - 45:00 | Build phase 1. Pairs in breakout rooms. COACH: Drop the starter project list link in chat. "Pairs. If you're comfortable, write your own prompt. If you're newer to AI, start with the starter prompt on screen — just fill in the blanks. Explaining it out loud to your partner catches problems. 12 minutes for your first run." Launch breakout rooms (pairs). Circulate. Common issues: prompts that are too vague, no example input, trying to do too much at once. For observers who aren't comfortable building yet: "Follow along with what your partner is doing. Take notes. You can try it at home with the sample prompts doc." At 40:00: "Five minutes left in this phase." At 44:00: "One minute. Run your prompt if you haven't."

Job Search Bootcamp

Build Phase 2: Refine and Improve

  1. Look at your first output. Identify one thing to improve.
  2. Adjust your prompt. Be more specific about what was missing.
  3. Run it again. Compare the two outputs.
  4. Repeat until you're satisfied with the result.

12 minutes. The refinement cycle is what separates a one-off prompt from a reusable tool.

45:00 - 57:00 | Build phase 2. Refinement. COACH: "Back in your pairs. Phase 2 is about making it better. Look at what your AI produced. What's missing? What's wrong? Fix one thing at a time." Circulate. Help people identify specific improvements rather than starting over. At 50:00: "Halfway through phase 2. If your prompt works, start thinking about edge cases." At 55:00: "Two minutes. Wrap up your refinement. We're moving to documentation next."

Writing a Great README

  • Problem: What manual process does this replace?
  • Solution: What does the workflow do, in plain language?
  • How to use it: Step-by-step instructions anyone can follow
