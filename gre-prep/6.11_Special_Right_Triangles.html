Part 1

45-45-90 Triangles

The isosceles right triangle

0:30-0:40 | Transition to 45-45-90 triangle.

45-45-90 Triangle

Ratio: $1 : 1 : \sqrt{2}$

Part Value Key Fact
Leg $x$ Two equal legs (isosceles)
Leg $x$ Two $45\degree$ angles
Hypotenuse $x\sqrt{2}$ Appears in square diagonals

Given Leg Hyp $= $ leg $\times \sqrt{2}$    Given Hyp Leg $= $ hyp $\div \sqrt{2}$

0:40-2:00 | The 45-45-90 ratio: 1, 1, root 2. Two equal legs, two 45-degree angles, and the hypotenuse is leg times root 2. This triangle appears whenever you cut a square diagonally.

45-45-90 Triangle

Working both directions

Example

Hypotenuse $= 6\sqrt{2}$

Hyp = 10

Leg $= \frac{10}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{10\sqrt{2}}{2} = $ $5\sqrt{2}$

Square Diagonal

Diagonal $= 14$, so side $= \frac{14}{\sqrt{2}} = $ $7\sqrt{2}$

Area Problem

Hyp $= 8$, leg $= 4\sqrt{2}$. Area $= \frac{1}{2}(4\sqrt{2})^2 = $ $16$

2:00-3:30 | Four examples. Given leg = 6: hyp = 6 root 2. Given hyp = 10: leg = 5 root 2 (rationalize by multiplying by root 2 over root 2). Square with diagonal 14: side = 7 root 2. Area of isosceles right triangle with hyp 8: leg = 4 root 2, area = (1/2)(4 root 2)^2 = 16.

30-60-90 Triangle

Ratio: $1 : \sqrt{3} : 2$

Part Opposite Angle Value
Short leg $30\degree$ $x$
Long leg $60\degree$ $x\sqrt{3}$
Hypotenuse $90\degree$ $2x$

Strategy Always find the short leg first, then scale to find the other sides.

Pythagorean Triples

Memorize the big three

Triple Verification Common Multiples

Speed Tip Spot two numbers from a triple? The third is instant. No square root needed.

6:10-7:20 | The big three Pythagorean triples: 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17. Multiples work too. If you spot two numbers from a triple, the third follows instantly with no calculation. GRE trap: the test loves disguising triples with scaling factors. If you see legs 1.5 and 2, that's a 3-4-5 scaled by 0.5, so the hypotenuse is 2.5. Always check if dividing by a common factor reveals a known triple.

Key Takeaways

Special right triangles

  • 45-45-90 ratio — $1 : 1 : \sqrt{2}$. Two equal legs, hypotenuse is leg times $\sqrt{2}$
  • 30-60-90 ratio — $1 : \sqrt{3} : 2$. Find the short leg first, then scale
  • 3-4-5 triple — Most common. Watch for multiples like 6-8-10, 9-12-15
  • 5-12-13 and 8-15-17 — Less common but still tested. 5-12-13 appears in tangent-radius problems
  • Recognize first, calculate second — Spotting patterns saves significant time on the GRE
