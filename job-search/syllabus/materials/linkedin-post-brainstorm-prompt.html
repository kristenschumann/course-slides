Session 3 Resource

LinkedIn Post Brainstorm Prompt

Generate compelling post ideas from your career story, then write using three proven formats that drive engagement.

The Brainstorm Prompt

Copy this prompt into ChatGPT or Claude. Replace the bracketed sections with your background, and you will get five tailored post ideas ready to draft.

I want to start posting on LinkedIn to build my professional brand during my job search. Help me brainstorm 5 post ideas based on my background.

MY BACKGROUND:
Current/recent role: [YOUR ROLE]
Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY]
Key accomplishments: [LIST 3-5 THINGS YOU'RE PROUD OF]
Skills/expertise: [YOUR TOP SKILLS]
Career transition (if applicable): [WHAT YOU'RE MOVING FROM/TO]
Interests/opinions: [WHAT DO YOU HAVE STRONG VIEWS ON IN YOUR FIELD?]

For each post idea, provide:
1. A compelling first line (the hook that makes people stop scrolling)
2. The post format (Insight Post, Career Story, or Industry Take)
3. A 2-sentence summary of what the post would cover
4. Why this post would resonate with hiring managers and peers

Three Post Formats That Work

These three formats consistently drive engagement on LinkedIn. Each one works because it delivers value while showcasing your expertise to hiring managers.

Format 1

Insight Post

Hook → Context → The Insight → Why It Matters → Question

Share a lesson from your work. This format positions you as someone who doesn't just do the work -- you think deeply about it. Pick a moment where you learned something counterintuitive, saved time, or changed your approach.

Example Hook
"I automated a 4-hour weekly report into a 10-minute process. Here's what that taught me about the difference between being busy and being productive..."
Format 2

Career Story

Hook → The Situation → What You Did → What Changed → The Lesson

Share a turning point or challenge. Career stories are the highest-engagement format on LinkedIn because they are relatable and human. Vulnerability wins -- talk about a failure, a pivot, or a moment of doubt.

Example Hook
"Two years ago, I was rejected from my dream job. It was the best thing that happened to my career. Here's why..."
Format 3

Industry Take

Bold Statement → Why Most Get It Wrong → Your Perspective → Evidence → Call to Discussion

Share a perspective on your field. This format works because it sparks debate and positions you as a thought leader. The key: don't be contrarian for the sake of it. Share a genuine belief backed by your experience.

Example Hook
"Hot take: Most companies don't have a data problem. They have a 'nobody knows what question to ask' problem..."

Posting Tips

  • Post Tuesday through Thursday, 8-10 AM in your timezone. This is when LinkedIn engagement peaks. Avoid weekends and Monday mornings when feeds are flooded.
  • Your first line is everything. It is the only thing people see before "...see more." Treat it like an email subject line -- if it does not spark curiosity, nobody clicks.
  • End with a question to drive comments. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards posts that generate conversation. A specific question ("What's your version of this?") outperforms a generic one ("Thoughts?").
  • Aim for 1 post per week. Consistency beats volume. One thoughtful post weekly builds more credibility than daily posts that feel rushed or forced.
  • Engage with 5-10 posts before and after you publish. Comment meaningfully on others' content in the 30 minutes surrounding your post. This signals to LinkedIn's algorithm that you are an active participant, not just a broadcaster.
