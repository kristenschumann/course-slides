The Consulting STAR Method

Adapting the classic framework for MBB interviews

1:30 - 1:35 | Transition to the consulting-adapted STAR method.

Break Into Management Consulting

STAR Time Allocation

S

Situation

20% of airtime. Just enough context for the story to make sense.

T

Task

10% of airtime. One sentence on your responsibility.

A

Action

60% of airtime. Specific steps, decisions, and reasoning.

R

Result

10% of airtime. Quantified outcomes, not "it went well."

1:35 - 2:45 | Lead with the answer before STAR. Top-down communication signals consulting thinking. Most candidates over-invest in Situation and rush through Action. Flip that ratio. Quantify results: "Revenue increased 15% quarter-over-quarter." The biggest mistake is three minutes of backstory and thirty seconds of action.

McKinsey PEI

The most structured behavioral interview in consulting

2:45 - 2:50 | Transition to the McKinsey Personal Experience Interview.

Break Into Management Consulting

Four PEI Dimensions

Connection

Persuading or influencing someone. Changing minds, navigating disagreement, building buy-in. (formerly Personal Impact)

Drive

Starting something new while overcoming major obstacles under time pressure. (formerly Entrepreneurial Drive)

Leadership

Leading a diverse team through a challenge. Empowering others, leveraging different perspectives. (formerly Inclusive Leadership)

Growth

Adapting to significant disruption, ambiguity, or critical feedback with resilience. (formerly Courageous Change)

2:50 - 4:30 | Each PEI session focuses on one dimension, one story, for 10-15 minutes. Expect 10-25 follow-up probes. McKinsey uses STARR: STAR plus Reflection. The interviewer wants emotional honesty and self-awareness. Stories that collapse after the fifth follow-up mean the candidate hasn't gone deep enough.

BCG Behavioral Interview

  • Flexible format with no fixed script like McKinsey's PEI
  • Multiple stories per session, broader coverage across competencies
  • Personality questions appear more frequently than at McKinsey
  • Values real-time thinking and authentic, unrehearsed responses
  • Stories should be internalized, not memorized word-for-word
4:30 - 5:05 | BCG goes broad where McKinsey goes deep. Interviewers have flexibility in what they ask. They follow the conversation. BCG values on-the-fly thinking, so stories must be internalized enough to adapt mid-answer.

Break Into Management Consulting

Bain Experience Interview

  • Heaviest emphasis on culture fit among all MBB firms
  • Personality questions are scored, not just small talk
  • Interviewers push back on your answers to test conviction
  • Expects data-driven, quantified results in every story
  • Assesses whether you'd thrive in Bain's collaborative environment
5:05 - 5:45 | Bain has the heaviest culture-fit component. Personality questions about hobbies and values are scored. Bain pressure-tests conviction by challenging your answers. Don't backpedal. Stand your ground and explain your reasoning.

5:45 - 6:30 | Tactical summary: McKinsey tests depth, BCG tests breadth and adaptability, Bain tests conviction and culture fit. If interviewing at all three, you need stories that flex across formats. Experienced hires should use professional stories; undergrads and MBAs can draw from extracurriculars.

Story Bank Blueprint

  • 8-10 deeply developed stories you know inside and out
  • Cover all competency areas: leadership, teamwork, resilience, conflict, initiative, adaptability
  • Draw from the last 2-3 years. Older stories need a clear reason.
  • Each story must survive 10-25 follow-ups without collapsing.
  • One story, multiple uses. Reframe emphasis for different questions.
6:30 - 7:30 | Build 8-10 stories, not 20 vague anecdotes. Cover leadership, teamwork, obstacles, failure, conflict, initiative, adaptability. A single well-crafted story can answer multiple question types by shifting emphasis. For McKinsey, prepare two stories per PEI dimension. Invest 2-6 hours initially, then practice with a partner. Key Takeaways

Mastering Fit Interviews

  • Fit carries equal weight to cases. Strong case performance with weak behavioral answers gets you rejected.
  • Adapt STAR for consulting. Lead with the answer, spend 60% on Action, and quantify every result.
  • Each MBB firm runs a different format. McKinsey drills deep. BCG goes broad. Bain tests culture fit and conviction.
  • Build a bank of 8-10 stories covering leadership, teamwork, resilience, conflict, initiative, and adaptability.
  • Start your inventory now. Write down your strongest experiences. Resource 5.3 develops them into interview-ready stories.
