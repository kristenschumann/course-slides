Section 1

Analytical Writing

The Task 30 minutes

You'll receive a prompt presenting an issue or claim. Your job is to take a position and support your argument with reasoning and examples.

What matters Clear structure, logical reasoning, and relevant examples. Not fancy vocabulary.

Scoring
0 - 6
Half-point increments

Scored separately from Verbal and Quant. Many programs weight it less heavily, but a very low score can raise questions.

1:30 - 3:00 | Explain the writing section. One essay, 30 minutes, scored 0-6. Emphasize that clear structure matters more than vocabulary. Separate score from Verbal/Quant.

Verbal Reasoning

Three Question Types

~50%

Reading Comprehension

  • + Short and long passages
  • + Main ideas and inferences
  • + Argument analysis
~25%

Text Completion

  • + 1, 2, or 3 blanks to fill
  • + Vocabulary + context clues
  • + All blanks must be correct
~25%

Sentence Equivalence

  • + One blank, six choices
  • + Pick TWO that create same meaning
  • + Tests synonyms + context

Key skills: Strong vocabulary and the ability to read carefully for context clues. Active reading to understand how passages are structured.

3:10 - 5:00 | Walk through the three verbal question types. Reading Comp is the biggest chunk at 50%. Text Completion and Sentence Equivalence each about 25%. Key skills are vocabulary and careful reading.

Quantitative Reasoning

Four Question Types

01

Quantitative Comparison

Compare Quantity A vs B. Decide if A is greater, B is greater, they're equal, or the relationship can't be determined.

02

Problem Solving

Standard math problems. Some are multiple choice with one answer, some let you select multiple answers.

03

Data Interpretation

Charts, graphs, and tables. Often come in sets of 2-3 questions about the same data.

04

Numeric Entry

Type your answer. No choices means no process of elimination. You have to calculate exactly.

Content areas: Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Data Analysis. Concepts you've likely seen before, tested in specific ways.

5:10 - 7:00 | Cover the four quant question types. QC is unique to the GRE. Problem Solving is standard math. Data Interpretation uses charts/graphs. Numeric Entry has no answer choices. Content areas are Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Data Analysis.

7:00 - 8:30 | Explain the adaptive mechanism. The GRE adapts at the section level, not question by question. Your performance on Section 1 determines the difficulty of Section 2. Doing well on Section 1 unlocks a harder Section 2 with a higher score ceiling.
