Section 3

Three Fatal Mistakes

4:45 | Transition into the three mistakes.

VC Recruiting Course

A fortune cookie is not a thesis

The Mistake

"I think AI is probably going to be important in healthcare, and there might be some interesting opportunities." That could apply to any sector by swapping two words. It is not specific enough to be useful.

The Fix

"AI-native clinical documentation tools will replace manual chart review at 80% of U.S. health systems within five years, driven by CMS reimbursement changes. The winners will integrate directly into EHR workflows." That is falsifiable. That is a thesis.

4:45 - 5:15 | Mistake 1: the hedge thesis. [Click] "AI is probably going to be important in healthcare." That is a fortune cookie. A thesis requires a specific claim about where value will accrue, why now, and what the winning company profile looks like. [Click] Force yourself to name companies, cite numbers, and make falsifiable predictions.

VC Recruiting Course

The partner already knows the headlines

The Mistake

"The AI market has been evolving rapidly since 2022 when ChatGPT launched. Since then, we have seen massive investment in foundation models..." 60-90 seconds of context before a single position.

The Fix

Assume the interviewer knows the headlines. One sentence of context: "Given the commoditization of foundation models over the last 18 months, I am focused on the application layer." Done. Move to your non-obvious take.

5:15 - 5:45 | Mistake 2: the history lesson. [Click] The partner lives in this market every day. When you provide context they already have, you signal you don't understand your audience. [Click] One sentence of context maximum. Then your non-obvious take.

VC Recruiting Course

If it matches every headline, it's not a thesis

The Mistake

"I'm really excited about AI." So is everyone. A thesis that matches the consensus is an observation, not a thesis. The value of a thesis is that it helps you see deals others miss.

The Fix

Use the "most people think X, but I believe Y" structure. "Most people think the AI opportunity is in bigger models. I believe it is in the infrastructure layer that makes existing models usable for specific enterprise workflows."

5:45 - 6:15 | Mistake 3: the crowd thesis. [Click] "I'm excited about AI" is not a thesis. It is an observation. [Click] The fix: "Most people think X, but I believe Y." That structure forces you into a differentiated position. It shows a partner that you think for yourself.

Handling Pushback in Real Time

VC Recruiting Course

A partner who pushes back is engaged

Pushback Type Example How to Handle
Market Size "Isn't that too small?" Cite specific TAM data and growth catalysts.
Timing "Why now? This has been tried." Point to the structural change. Be specific.
Competition "How does this survive against [X]?" Explain the startup's structural advantage.
Contrarian "I disagree with your premise." Acknowledge, restate evidence, name what you would track.

VC Recruiting Course

Don't get defensive. Show your depth.

Best Response

"That is actually the risk I spend the most time thinking about." Shows intellectual honesty and analytical depth at the same time.

Honest Response

"I have not looked at it from that angle. My instinct is [X], but I would want to dig into [Y] before I would be confident." Authenticity wins.

Worst Response

Doubling down without addressing their point. Or bluffing through an answer you clearly do not have. Partners know when you are faking it.

7:00 - 7:30 | The single most important rule: do not get defensive. [Click] The best response shows you have already considered the objection. [Click] If you genuinely have not considered their point, say so. Honesty under pressure is more impressive than a polished deflection. [Click] The worst response is doubling down or bluffing. Partners have seen thousands of pitches. They know.

VC Recruiting Course

Key takeaways

  • Lead with the thesis, not the context. Your interviewer already knows the headlines. Start with your non-obvious take. SCPD: Sector, Conviction, Proof, Deal. 90 seconds.
  • Tailor your delivery to your audience. A partner wants judgment. A principal wants rigor. An operating partner wants founder empathy. Same thesis, different emphasis.
  • Kill the hedge thesis. If your thesis cannot be wrong, it is not a thesis. Name companies, cite numbers, make falsifiable predictions. That separates candidates from tourists.
  • Treat pushback as a feature, not a bug. When a partner challenges your thesis, they are giving you a chance to demonstrate real-time thinking. Be honest about what you have and have not considered.
