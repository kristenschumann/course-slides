Signal Words Are Your Roadmap

Every Text Completion and Sentence Equivalence question contains hidden directions. Signal words tell you exactly what kind of answer to look for.

Learn to spot these words, and you'll know whether your answer should match the clue, contrast with it, or show cause and effect.

Today's focus: Master the four categories of signal words that appear on every GRE verbal section.

Same-Direction Signals

These words tell you the blank matches, continues, or reinforces the clue.

and moreover similarly indeed thus : (colon) likewise in fact also

Example

"The research was thorough and _______, leaving no aspect of the topic unexamined."

"And" tells us the blank should match "thorough." Prediction: comprehensive, exhaustive, meticulous.

The Colon Rule

"The CEO was known for her _______: she never missed a deadline or forgot a commitment."

A colon introduces an explanation or definition. What follows explains the blank. Prediction: reliability, dependability.

Contrast Signals

These words signal that the blank opposes or contradicts the clue. Predict the opposite.

but however although despite while whereas yet nevertheless rather than

Example

"Although the novel received critical acclaim, its _______ sales disappointed the publisher."

"Although" signals contrast with "critical acclaim." Prediction: poor, weak, disappointing.

Tricky Contrast

"The historian was _______, whereas her colleague rushed to publish incomplete findings."

"Whereas" contrasts with "rushed." Prediction: careful, methodical, patient.

Cause/Effect Signals

These words show a logical relationship. The blank often results from or explains the clue.

because since therefore consequently as a result thus so leads to

Cause to Effect

"Because the evidence was overwhelming, the jury reached a _______ verdict."

"Because" links overwhelming evidence to the verdict. Prediction: unanimous, quick, decisive.

Effect from Cause

"The musician's _______ practice schedule led to her flawless performance."

"Led to" shows the blank caused the flawless performance. Prediction: rigorous, disciplined, intense.

Intensifiers and Qualifiers

These words modify the degree of your prediction. They tell you whether to pick a stronger or weaker word.

Intensifiers (Stronger)

even especially particularly extremely

"Even her critics admitted she was _______."
Prediction: extremely talented, exceptional

Qualifiers (Softer)

somewhat rather fairly slightly

"The proposal was somewhat _______."
Prediction: flawed, problematic (not terrible)

Watch the degree: "Somewhat controversial" and "extremely controversial" require different answer choices. Intensifiers and qualifiers help you pick the right strength.

Complete Worked Example

"Although the scientist's theory was initially dismissed as too _______, subsequent research proved that her predictions were remarkably accurate."

1
READ FOR STRUCTURE

Theory dismissed as something, but later proved accurate. The sentence shows a turnaround.

2
FIND SIGNAL WORDS

"Although" = contrast signal
"proved" = cause/effect

3
LOCATE CLUE

Clue: "remarkably accurate"
"Although" tells us blank contrasts with accuracy.

4
PREDICT

Opposite of accurate = unreliable, far-fetched, speculative, unfounded

innovative speculative practical methodical timely

Answer: Speculative. The theory was dismissed as "speculative" (unreliable/uncertain) but proved accurate. This matches our prediction and fits the contrast signal.
