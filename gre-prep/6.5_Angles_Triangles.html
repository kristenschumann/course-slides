Angle Relationships

Setting up equations

If one angle is 25°, what is the complement?

$90\degree - 25\degree = $ $65\degree$

One angle is 3 times the other. Find both.

$x + 3x = 180\degree$, so $x = $ $45\degree$ and $3x = $ $135\degree$

2:00-2:40 | Two quick examples. Complementary: if one angle is 25, the other is 90 - 25 = 65. Supplementary with a ratio: x + 3x = 180, so x = 45 and 3x = 135.

Parallel Lines

Transversal creates two angle sizes

When a transversal crosses parallel lines, eight angles form in just two sizes.

2:40-3:20 | Parallel lines cut by a transversal: the key insight is only two angle sizes exist. Here you can see all eight angles are either 70 or 110 degrees. GRE speed tip: once you find one angle, you know all eight. If you see any angle is x, the others are x or (180 - x). Don't waste time identifying "alternate interior" vs "corresponding" -- just use the two-sizes rule.

Parallel Lines

Equal vs supplementary angles

Equal Angles

Corresponding, alternate interior, alternate exterior

Supplementary

Same-side interior angles add to $180\degree$

3:20-4:00 | Corresponding, alternate interior, and alternate exterior are all equal. Same-side interior angles are supplementary. If one angle is 70, the others are 70 or 110.

Example 1

Solving with parallel lines

Problem

Parallel lines cut by a transversal. Alternate interior angles are $(2x + 10)\degree$ and $(3x - 20)\degree$. Find $x$.

Solution

Alternate interior angles are equal.

$2x + 10 = 3x - 20$

Answer: $x =$ $30$

4:00-4:40 | Algebra with parallel lines: alternate interior angles are equal, so set them equal. 2x + 10 = 3x - 20, solve to get x = 30.

Triangle Rules

Interior and exterior angles

Interior Sum

$\angle A + \angle B + \angle C = 180\degree$

$40\degree + 75\degree + x = 180\degree$, so $x = $ $65\degree$

Exterior Angle

Equals sum of two remote interiors

Exterior $= 50\degree + 70\degree = $ $120\degree$

4:50-6:00 | Two key rules. Interior angles always sum to 180. An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles. A triangle cannot have two right angles.

Triangle Inequality

When can three sides form a triangle?

The longest side must be less than the sum of the other two sides.

Invalid

Sides 3, 7, 12

$12 < 3 + 7 = 10$? No

Valid

Sides 5, 8, 11

$11 < 5 + 8 = 13$? Yes

6:00-7:10 | Triangle inequality: the longest side must be less than the sum of the other two. 3, 7, 12 fails because 12 is not less than 10. 5, 8, 11 works because 11 is less than 13. Range rule: if sides are 5 and 9, the third side must be between 4 and 14. GRE tip: QC questions love asking "which of these could be the third side?" Use the range formula: |a - b| < c < a + b. This eliminates wrong answers instantly.

Triangle Properties

Side-angle relationships

Largest angle is opposite the longest side. Smallest angle is opposite the shortest side.

From Sides

Sides: 5, 8, 10

Angles: $\angle$ opp 5 $<$ $\angle$ opp 8 $<$ $\angle$ opp 10

From Angles

$\angle P = 50\degree$, $\angle Q = 60\degree$, $\angle R = 70\degree$

Longest side is $PQ$ (opposite largest $\angle R$)

7:10-8:10 | In any triangle, the largest angle sits opposite the longest side. If you know side lengths, you can order the angles. If you know angles, you can order the sides. This is a common GRE testing point.

Example 2

Isosceles triangle angles

Problem

In isosceles triangle ABC, $AB = BC$ and $\angle A = 40\degree$. Find $\angle B$ and $\angle C$.

Solution

Equal sides $\rightarrow$ equal base angles: $\angle A = \angle C = 40\degree$

$\angle B = 180\degree - 40\degree - 40\degree$

Answer: $\angle B =$ $100\degree$

8:10-9:00 | Isosceles triangle: two equal sides means two equal base angles. AB = BC so angle A = angle C = 40. Angle B = 180 - 40 - 40 = 100.

Key Takeaways

Angles & triangle rules

  • Vertical angles: Always equal. Linear pairs sum to $180\degree$
  • Parallel lines + transversal: Creates only two angle sizes (equal or supplementary)
  • Triangle interior angles: Always sum to $180\degree$
  • Exterior angle theorem: Equals the sum of two remote interior angles
  • Triangle inequality & side-angle rule: Longest side less than sum of other two; largest angle opposite longest side
9:00-10:00 | Summary: vertical angles equal, parallel transversals create two sizes, triangle angles sum to 180, exterior angle equals the remote interiors, triangle inequality checks validity, largest angle opposite longest side.
