Exponent Rules

The Three Things That Matter

Every GRE exponent question tests your ability to eliminate these three obstacles:

Unfactored Numbers

18, 27, 80... numbers that hide their prime bases.

Plus/Minus Signs

You cannot add or subtract different exponents directly.

Denominators

Fractions complicate bases. Flip them with negative exponents.

Get the bases the same

Unfactored Numbers

Vertical Factoring

For perfect squares, cubes, etc., rewrite as a prime base raised to a power. Then apply the power-to-power rule.

The Rule

$(a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}$

Raise a power to a power: multiply exponents.

Combine

$a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$

Same base: keep the base, add exponents.

Example: $27^2 \times 9^3 = (3^3)^2 \times (3^2)^3 = 3^6 \times 3^6 = $ $3^{12}$

1:25-2:30 | Vertical factoring: rewrite numbers as prime powers. 27 is 3 cubed, 9 is 3 squared. Apply the power-to-power rule: multiply the exponents. Then once bases match, add exponents. 3^6 times 3^6 equals 3^12.

Unfactored Numbers

Dragging Around the Exponent

Problem

$18^5$ is equivalent to which?

I. $(2)^5 + (9)^5$

II. $2^5 \times 9^5$

III. $2^5 \times 3^5 \times 3^5$

Solution

Step 1: Factor the base: $18 = 2 \times 3 \times 3$

Step 2: Attach exponent to each factor: $18^5 = 2^5 \times 3^5 \times 3^5$

Key: You cannot distribute exponents over addition (I is wrong)

Answer: III only

2:30-3:30 | Dragging around the exponent: break 18 into its prime factors 2, 3, 3, then attach the exponent to each factor. 18^5 equals 2^5 times 3^5 times 3^5. Roman numeral I is wrong because you cannot distribute exponents over addition. Answer is III only.

Example 1

Complex Base Matching

Problem

$32^{5n-2} \times 2^{5n-3} \times 15^2 = 900$

What is $n$?

Solution

Step 1: $32 = 2^5$, so $32^{5n-2} = 2^{25n-10}$

Step 2: $15^2 = 5^2 \times 3^2$; $900 = 2^2 \times 3^2 \times 5^2$

Step 3: Cancel $5^2 \times 3^2$: $2^{25n-10} \times 2^{5n-3} = 2^2$

Step 4: $30n - 13 = 2$, so $n =$ $\frac{1}{2}$

3:30-5:00 | Full walkthrough of the hook problem. Step 1: rewrite 32 as 2^5, apply power-to-power. Step 2: factor 15^2 and 900 into primes. Step 3: cancel the 5^2 and 3^2 from both sides. Step 4: add exponents on the left, set equal to 2, solve for n = 1/2.

Horizontal factoring

Plus/Minus Signs

Horizontal Factoring

To add or subtract exponents, you need both the base and the exponent to match.

Expression Can Combine? Result
$5x^3 + 3x^3$ Yes $8x^3$
$5x^5 + 3y^3$ No (different bases) Cannot simplify
$x^5 - x^3$ No (different exponents) Factor: $x^3(x^2 - 1)$

When you cannot combine, factor out the smaller power.

5:10-6:00 | The rule for adding and subtracting exponents: both base and exponent must match. If they don't, factor out the smaller power. This is called horizontal factoring.

Example 2

Factor the Numerator

Problem

$\frac{19^3 - 3(19^2)}{19^2}$

Solution

Step 1: Factor numerator: $19^2(19 - 3) = 19^2 \times 16$

Step 2: Cancel: $\frac{19^2 \times 16}{19^2}$

Answer: $16$

Watch Out

You cannot cancel $19^2$ from individual terms linked by subtraction. Factor first, then cancel.

6:00-6:50 | Example 2: factor the numerator. You cannot cancel 19s when they are connected by a minus sign. Factor out 19^2, get 19^2 times 16, then cancel the 19^2 from the denominator. Answer is 16.

Example 3

Repeated Addition of Exponents

Problem

$2^{K+1} + 2^{K+1} + 2^{K+1} + 2^{K+1}$

Solution

Step 1: Factor: $2^{K+1}(1+1+1+1) = 2^{K+1} \times 4$

Step 2: Get bases same: $4 = 2^2$

Step 3: Add exponents: $2^{K+1} \times 2^2 = 2^{K+3}$

Answer: $2^{K+3}$

6:50-7:40 | Example 3: four identical terms means factor out the common term and multiply by 4. Then rewrite 4 as 2^2 and add exponents. Answer is 2^(K+3).

Example 4

Greatest Prime Factor

Problem

What is the greatest prime factor of $9^{17} - 27^{10}$?

Solution

Step 1: Vertical factor: $9^{17} = 3^{34}$, $27^{10} = 3^{30}$

Step 2: Horizontal factor: $3^{30}(3^4 - 1) = 3^{30} \times 80$

Step 3: Factor 80: $80 = 2^4 \times 5$

Greatest prime factor: $5$

7:40-8:40 | Example 4: combines both techniques. First vertical factor to get bases the same as powers of 3. Then horizontal factor because of the minus sign. Factor out 3^30, leaving 3^4 minus 1 which is 80. Factor 80 into 2^4 times 5. The prime factors are 2, 3, and 5. Greatest is 5.

Denominators

Negative Exponents

Flipping the sign on an exponent flips numerator and denominator.

Expression Rewrite Value
$4^{-2}$ $\frac{1}{4^2}$ $\frac{1}{16}$
$\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{-3}$ $2^3$ $8$
$x^{-n}$ $\frac{1}{x^n}$ Flip to denominator
8:50-9:30 | Negative exponents flip the fraction. 4 to the negative 2 is 1 over 16. One-half to the negative 3 is 8. This is how we eliminate denominators instantly.

Example 5

Eliminating Denominators

Problem

$\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{12} \times \left(\frac{1}{27}\right)^k = \left(\frac{1}{18}\right)^{12}$

What is $k$?

Solution

Step 1: Flip signs: $2^{-12} \times 3^{-3k} = 18^{-12}$

Step 2: Expand: $18^{-12} = 2^{-12} \times 3^{-24}$

Step 3: Cancel $2^{-12}$: $3^{-3k} = 3^{-24}$

Step 4: $-3k = -24$, so $k =$ $8$

9:30-11:00 | Example 5: use negative exponents to eliminate all fractions. Flip exponent signs so 1/2 becomes 2, 1/27 becomes 27 which is 3^3, and 1/18 becomes 18 which is 2 times 3^2. Cancel the 2^-12 from both sides. Match the powers of 3: -3k equals -24, so k equals 8.

Summary

Exponent Rules

  • Unfactored numbers - Get bases the same using vertical factoring or dragging the exponent
  • Plus/minus signs - Use horizontal factoring to pull out the smaller power
  • Denominators - Flip the sign on the exponent to eliminate fractions
  • Power-to-power - $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$; multiply the exponents
  • Same base, multiply - $a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$; add the exponents
  • The key principle - Every GRE exponent problem is about eliminating these three obstacles

Vertical factoring and dragging the exponent handle unfactored numbers. Horizontal factoring handles plus/minus signs. Negative exponents handle denominators. Once these obstacles are eliminated, the algebra is simple.
