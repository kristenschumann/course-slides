MBA vs. Job Resume

What Admissions Committees Care About

Adcoms care about leadership and impact, not technical skills or industry jargon. They want to see you zoom out from individual contributor to higher-level business impact.

Engineers get into business school by proving they see the broader vision of why their work helped the organization, not by proving they are the best engineer. Translate technical accomplishments into business results.

1:00 - 2:15 | This is the single biggest shift I coach my clients through. When you apply for engineering roles, you lead with technical depth â€” frameworks, system design, performance metrics. For business school, adcoms do not care about that. They care about leadership and impact. They want to know why the CEO cared about your work. ðŸ’¡ I had a client who was so proud of improving ML model processing time by 150 percent. Great engineering achievement. But I told him: the adcom does not know what that means. What they need to hear is that his improvements saved the business 5 million dollars annually. That is the translation. Every technical accomplishment has a business story behind it â€” my job is helping you find it.

Resume Structure

The Four Sections

01

Education

GPA, scholarships, awards, campus involvement. Show academics plus community engagement.

02

Experience

The core of your resume. Reverse chronological. Focus on results and impact, not responsibilities.

03

Service & Leadership

Volunteer work, community projects, nonprofit roles. Show a pattern of service and initiative.

04

Skills & Interests

Add personality. Highlight achievements over labels. "5x Music Award" beats "Cello player."

2:15 - 3:45 | I structure every client resume around these four sections. Education: GPA, scholarships, awards, but also campus involvement. Engineers sometimes leave this sparse â€” do not. Schools want to see you will be an active community member, not just a student. Experience: the heart of your resume. Reverse chronological, results-driven. This is where we spend the most time together. Service and Leadership: this is where engineers often have the thinnest section, and it matters more than you think. Schools like HBS literally say they "educate leaders who make a difference in the world." ðŸ’¡ I always ask my clients to dig into volunteer work, mentoring, open source contributions, anything that shows initiative beyond your day job. Skills and Interests: this is your personality section. Achievements over labels. "5x Superior Cello Music Award" tells a story. "Cello player for 10 years" does not.

Part 2

Writing Strong Bullet Points

Results over responsibilities

3:45 - 3:50 | This next part is where I see the biggest transformations with my clients. Strong bullet points are the difference between a forgettable resume and one that makes an adcom stop and pay attention.

Bullet Points

Weak vs. Strong: Software Engineer

Weak

  • Pitched product to investors to get grants
  • Managed inventory and supply for main product
  • Helped patients remember to take pills through new technology

Strong

  • Won $65K grant by pitching to investors for program with <2% acceptance
  • Led team of 5 to source $10K+ in product and build 2,000-person pre-order list
  • Increased pill compliance from 55% to 95%+ through patented technology

Start with strong action verbs. Quantify impact with specific numbers. Keep each bullet to one line when possible.

Bullet Points

Career Framing

Three Profiles Schools Look For

School-Specific Fit

Align your resume to each school's unique emphases, priorities, and specialties.

High-Potential Path

Established career in consulting, banking, or tech. Complement with your unique story and interests.

Diverse Background

Non-traditional path with fresh ideas. Prove you are grounded, realistic, and high-impact.

Formatting

Rules to Follow

  • One page maximum: No exceptions. Every word should be intentional.
  • Consistent formatting: Same font, spacing, and bullet style throughout.
  • Reverse chronological: Most recent experience first within each section.
  • No objective statements: Skip summaries and objectives. Let your content speak.
7:45 - 9:00 | Formatting seems basic, but ðŸ’¡ I catch these mistakes constantly. One page maximum, no exceptions. Engineers sometimes want to list every project and technology â€” resist that urge. Every word earns its spot. Consistent formatting throughout. Same font, same spacing, same bullet style. Adcoms read hundreds of resumes; inconsistency signals carelessness. Reverse chronological within each section. And no objective statements or professional summaries â€” they waste prime real estate. Keep the layout clean and scannable. No fancy design. Submit as PDF unless the school says otherwise.

Review Process

Before You Submit

Read your resume from the adcom's perspective. Remove details they will not care about. Draw out points that relate to your career path and each specific school.

Ask trusted colleagues and mentors to review it. You may need slightly different versions for each school. A powerful resume grounds the story you share in essays and recommendations.
