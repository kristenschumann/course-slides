The Core Equation

Revenue

Price x Volume. Decompose further by business model: customers x revenue per customer, or product mix across lines.

Costs

Fixed + Variable. Fixed costs stay constant in the short term. Variable costs scale directly with production volume.

Profit = Revenue - Costs. With just four branches (price, volume, fixed costs, variable costs), you can map every profitability problem.

Three Ways to Decompose Revenue

Price x Volume

Best for single-product businesses. A steel manufacturer sells X tons at $Y per ton.

Customers x Revenue

Best for subscriptions and services. Isolates acquisition vs. retention vs. monetization.

Product Mix

Best for multi-product businesses. Sum of volume x price across all product lines.

1:15 - 2:00 | Three revenue decomposition methods. Price x Volume for single-product. Customers x Revenue per Customer for SaaS and subscriptions. Product Mix for multi-product businesses. Always match the method to the business model.

Break Into Management Consulting

Fixed vs. Variable Costs

Fixed Costs

  • Rent, executive salaries, insurance
  • Depreciation, loan payments
  • Do not change with output volume
  • Can change through restructuring

Variable Costs

  • Raw materials, direct labor, packaging
  • Shipping, sales commissions
  • Scale directly with volume
  • Expressed as cost per unit
2:00 - 2:45 | Fixed costs don't change with volume in the short term. Variable costs scale with output. The diagnostic question: did total cost increase because fixed costs rose, variable cost per unit rose, or volume increased pushing up total variable costs? Segment further by business type.

Ask before you analyze

Break Into Management Consulting

Five Clarifying Questions

  • Timeframe. When did the decline start? Sudden drop or gradual trend?
  • Magnitude. By how much have profits declined? A 5% dip or a 50% collapse?
  • Scope. All product lines and regions, or concentrated in one area?
  • Relative Performance. Are competitors experiencing the same decline?
  • Definition of Profit. Operating profit, net profit, or margins?
2:50 - 4:00 | Five questions to ask before touching the tree. Timeframe is the most forgotten and changes everything. Magnitude tells you if it's one big factor or death by a thousand cuts. Scope narrows the investigation. Relative performance reveals internal vs. external causes. Definition of profit drives the analysis direction.

Diagnosing the Root Cause

A systematic process for working the tree

4:00 - 4:05 | Transition to the diagnostic process.

Break Into Management Consulting

The Diagnostic Process

  • Start broad. Ask whether the decline is driven by revenue or costs.
  • Confirm before going deeper. Verify the branch contains the problem.
  • Benchmark. Compare to historical performance and competitors.
  • Quantify the gap. Put a dollar number on the root cause.
  • Test multiple hypotheses. Don't anchor on the first explanation.
4:05 - 5:15 | Five-step diagnostic process. Start broad to eliminate half the tree. Confirm before diving into any branch. Benchmark against competitors and history. Quantify the gap with specific dollar figures. Test multiple hypotheses before concluding. MBA candidates should expect more complex multi-segment cases.

RetailCo: Recommendation

Seven Traps to Avoid

Common mistakes that derail profitability cases

7:00 - 7:05 | Transition to seven common traps.

Break Into Management Consulting

Profitability Case Traps

  • Jumping to solutions before diagnosing the root cause
  • Forgetting timeframe, which changes the entire diagnosis
  • Aggregate-level analysis only, missing problems hidden in segments
  • Ignoring the mix shift when revenue looks flat in total
  • Treating all costs as controllable (leases, union contracts are not)
  • Not quantifying your recommendation with specific dollar figures
  • Confusing margin decline with profit decline, two different problems
7:05 - 8:15 | Seven traps: jumping to solutions, forgetting timeframe, only analyzing aggregates, ignoring mix shift, assuming all costs are controllable, not quantifying recommendations, confusing margin decline with profit decline.

Essential Calculations

Tool Formula When to Use
Break-Even Fixed Costs / (Price - Variable Cost per Unit) New product launch or business unit viability
Contribution Margin Price - Variable Cost per Unit Evaluate whether to keep a product line
Revenue Bridge Prior Revenue +/- Price +/- Volume +/- Mix Explain revenue changes period over period
8:15 - 8:40 | Three essential calculations. Break-even gives you the volume needed to cover fixed costs. Contribution margin tells you how much each unit contributes. Revenue bridge isolates the impact of price, volume, and mix changes separately. These are what real consultants use on engagements.

Key Takeaways

What to Remember

  • Highest-ROI case type. Profit = Revenue - Costs applies to virtually every business and every interview.
  • Ask five questions first. Timeframe, magnitude, scope, relative performance, and definition of profit.
  • The tree is diagnostic, not a checklist. Find the broken branch and go deep on that branch.
  • Watch for the mix shift. Flat revenue does not mean revenue is fine if the product mix changed.
  • Always quantify. "Reduce COGS by 8-12% to recover $4M annually" beats "costs are too high."
8:40 - 9:00 | Five takeaways: highest-ROI case type, ask five questions before the tree, tree is diagnostic not a checklist, watch for mix shift, always quantify recommendations with dollars.
