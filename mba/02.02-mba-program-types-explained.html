Core Formats

The Three Main Paths

Full-Time

2 years, full immersion. Best for career changers with 3-7 years of experience. Maximum recruiting access.

Part-Time

Evenings and weekends, up to 5 years. Keep your job and salary. Best for advancing within your current path.

Executive

18-24 months, intensive modules. For leaders with 10+ years of experience. Often employer-sponsored.

1:15 - 2:45 | The three main paths. Full-time is two years of full immersion. You leave your job and go all in. Ideal if you have 3 to 7 years of experience and want to make a significant career change. You get full recruiting access, an internship between years, and over 90% of scholarships go to full-time students. Part-time programs are evenings and weekends, lasting up to five years. You keep your job and salary. Best if you want to advance within your current company or industry, not pivot entirely. Application growth for part-time programs has actually outpaced full-time recently. Executive MBA is 18 to 24 months of intensive modules. Designed for people with 10 or more years of experience who are already in or near leadership roles. 69% of EMBA applicants have 10-plus years of experience. Employers frequently sponsor tuition.

Full-Time Deep Dive

The Investment Is Significant

Total cost at a top program: $100K to $300K including tuition, living expenses, and foregone salary. Over 90% of scholarships go to full-time students.

Choose full-time if you want to change careers, need on-campus recruiting access, and can afford the two-year investment. The internship between years often leads directly to your full-time offer.

4:15 - 5:30 | Let's go deeper on full-time. The investment is significant. Total cost at a top program runs $100K to $300K depending on school, location, and living expenses, and that includes foregone salary. But over 90% of scholarships go to full-time students, so the net cost can be much lower. Stanford GSB offers 120+ electives, 9 core courses, and a global experience requirement. That depth is hard to match in other formats. The internship between years is a major advantage: it often leads directly to your full-time offer. Choose full-time if you want to change careers, need recruiting access, and can financially manage the two-year commitment.

While You Work

Part-Time vs. Executive

Part-Time MBA

Experience: 5-10 years

Duration: 2.5-5 years (evenings/weekends)

Key benefit: Same degree, no income gap.

  • Keep your salary and benefits throughout.
  • Apply learnings to your job immediately.
  • 56-60% of programs saw application growth.

Tradeoff: Less immersive experience and slower timeline.

Executive MBA

Experience: 10+ years

Duration: 18-24 months (intensive modules)

Key benefit: Peer network of senior leaders.

  • Classmates are VPs, directors, and C-suite.
  • GMAT often waived for experienced candidates.
  • Employers frequently sponsor tuition.

Tradeoff: $150K-$220K+ cost and demanding schedule.

5:30 - 7:15 | Part-time versus executive. Part-time works best with 5 to 10 years of experience. No income gap, you keep your salary and benefits. At the end, you have the same degree as full-time students. Top programs offer 100+ electives, identical to full-time. If your employer pays, you might graduate debt-free. 56% of weekend programs and 60% of evening programs reported application growth recently. The tradeoff is a less immersive experience and a slower timeline. Executive MBA is for people with 10+ years already in or near leadership. Your classmates are VPs, directors, and C-suite leaders. The peer network alone can be transformative. GMAT is often waived for experienced candidates. Cost runs $150K to $220K+, but employer sponsorship is common. The tradeoff is the cost and the demanding schedule of intensive modules alongside your full-time job.

Alternative Formats

Four More Paths to Consider

  • Online MBA: Learn from anywhere. Many top schools don't note "online" on transcripts.
  • Deferred enrollment: Apply as a student, defer 2-5 years. Lock in admission early.
  • Specialized MBA: Industry-focused (tech, healthcare, finance). Often STEM-designated.
  • Dual degree: JD/MBA, MD/MBA, MBA/MPP in 3-5 years. Bridge multiple fields.
7:20 - 9:30 | Four alternative formats. Online MBA offers maximum flexibility. Michigan Ross includes optional in-person residencies alongside fully online coursework. Many programs don't disclose online status on transcripts, the credential is the same. The concern that online MBAs don't count has largely faded. Deferred enrollment is newer. You apply as an undergraduate or master's student, get accepted, then defer 2 to 5 years while gaining experience. Northwestern Kellogg's Future Leaders Program requires no test scores. These are highly competitive with lower acceptance rates than regular programs. Specialized MBAs focus on industries like tech, healthcare, or finance. NYU Stern's Tech MBA is a one-year, STEM-designated program. Great if you have a clear industry focus. Dual degrees combine an MBA with law, medicine, or policy. Harvard offers JD/MBA, MD/MBA, MBA/MPP, and several MBA/MS combinations. Typically 3 to 5 years, shorter than pursuing degrees separately.

Common Misconceptions

Myths Worth Challenging

"Full-time is most prestigious"

Prestige depends on the school, not the format. A part-time Booth MBA carries more weight than a full-time degree from a lower-ranked program.

"Online MBAs don't count"

Outdated. Michigan Ross, Berkeley Haas, and other top schools offer respected online programs. Employers evaluate the brand.

"EMBA is just for old people"

It is for experienced people. If you are a senior professional, you want peers at your level in the classroom.

"Specialized MBAs are too narrow"

They are narrow by design. If you know your industry, that focus is an advantage, not a limitation.

9:30 - 10:45 | Four misconceptions to challenge. "Full-time is always the most prestigious." Prestige depends on the school, not the format. A part-time Booth MBA carries more weight than a full-time degree from a lower-ranked program. "Online MBAs don't count." Outdated. Top schools like Michigan Ross and Berkeley Haas offer strong online programs. Employers increasingly evaluate the school brand and your skills, not the delivery format. "EMBA is just for old people." It is for experienced people. You want to be in a classroom with peers at your level. And "specialized MBAs are too narrow." They are narrow by design. If you know your industry, that focus is an asset.

Decision Framework

Match Format to Your Situation

By Goal

  • Career change → Full-Time
  • Advance in role → Part-Time or Online
  • Senior leadership → EMBA
  • Lock in early → Deferred
  • Deep expertise → Specialized
  • Bridge fields → Dual Degree

By Constraint

  • Can't leave job → Part-Time, EMBA, Online
  • Can't relocate → Online
  • Limited budget → Online or Part-Time
  • Employer sponsors → Part-Time or EMBA
  • Still in school → Deferred
10:45 - 12:00 | Here is the decision framework. Start with your primary goal. Career change points to full-time. Advancing in your current role points to part-time or online. Moving into senior leadership points to EMBA. Locking in admission early means deferred. Deep industry expertise means specialized. Bridging multiple disciplines means dual degree. Then layer in your constraints. Cannot leave your job? Part-time, EMBA, or online. Cannot relocate? Online. Limited budget? Online or part-time with employer support. Employer willing to sponsor? Part-time or EMBA. Still a student? Deferred enrollment.
